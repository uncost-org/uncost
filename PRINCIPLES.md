# Working principles

## 1. Receipts first

Every public number, claim, and cost estimate should be sourced, dated, confidence-labeled, and open to correction. Derived figures identify every contributing source. Scenarios remain visibly distinct from observed facts.

## 2. Open and reusable

Models, methods, dashboards, and project designs should be public and reusable wherever rights, safety, privacy, and partner terms allow. Open tools do not mean open personal data, open brand rights, or disregard for third-party licences.

## 3. Nonpartisan practical optimism

Uncost is bold but phased, lawful, measurable, and credible across political identities. It studies systems, costs, incentives, and mechanisms without endorsing candidates or parties.

## Operating commitments

- **Cost reduction is the outcome.** Data and content are means, not the destination.
- **Humans remain accountable.** AI can assist research, drafting, design, and operations; humans approve public claims, money, permissions, safety decisions, and legal commitments.
- **Negative results count.** If an intervention does not lower total cost, publish that result.
- **Money does not buy governance.** Donations do not buy votes, conclusions, coverage, or priority.
- **Sensitive work stays gated.** Healthcare, care, child-facing education, vulnerable-person work, and physical pilots require heightened review.
- **Local people and qualified partners act.** Uncost publishes evidence, software, models, and playbooks; it does not quietly turn a study into a regulated service.
- **Dates are targets, not pressure overrides.** Stage order and evidence gates outrank calendar ambition.
