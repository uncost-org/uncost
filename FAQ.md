# Frequently asked questions

## What is Uncost?

A nonprofit, nonpartisan movement to lower the real cost of living through evidence, open-source tools, practical AI and robotics pathways, and community action.

## Is Uncost trying to abolish money?

No. The grounded aim is to make essential living needs less dependent on money by lowering real costs. Uncost does not claim every cost disappears.

## Is it a political party?

No. Uncost is strictly nonpartisan. It may publish sourced public-interest analysis, but it does not support or oppose candidates, parties, or campaigns.

## Is it a DAO, token, or crypto project?

No. The Assembly uses no tokens, on-chain voting, or crypto mechanics.

## What is being built first?

A source library, regional methodology, first receipts-complete cost visual, and the three-part Human Essentials Dashboard system: PRJ-002 data, PRJ-003 scenarios, and PRJ-001 presentation.

## Why only Shelter, Food, and Energy first?

All fifteen sectors matter. These three provide a focused first proof before expanding. Starting narrow reduces stale data, weak methodology, and overpromising.

## Are donations open?

Not as tax-deductible donations. That waits for fiscal-sponsor confirmation and the required controls.

## Are the policies active?

No. They are public drafts pending independent Claude review, founder review, and legal/sponsor review where required. Publication is not adoption.

## Does Uncost provide healthcare, care, education, safety, engineering, or legal services?

No. Sensitive areas remain research/study lanes and require qualified partners and heightened safeguards before any pilot or service could be considered.

## Can AI publish or operate Uncost by itself?

No. AI may research, draft, summarize, design, and model. Humans remain accountable for public claims, funds, permissions, personal data, safety decisions, moderation, and legal commitments.

## How can I contribute?

Review [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md). Early useful roles include source review, data methodology, accessibility, privacy, nonprofit compliance, translation, design, and focused project research.
