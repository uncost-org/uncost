# The Assembly

The Assembly is the intended transparent supporter voice of Uncost. Supporters should be able to propose ideas, prioritize work, raise objections, and vote on priorities and proposed changes, with proposals and outcomes recorded publicly.

## Current status

The Assembly is **not operational**. No formal vote runs until POL-006 has completed independent Claude review, founder review, legal and fiscal-sponsor review where applicable, and formal adoption.

## Intended direction

Where law and governance allow, Assembly outcomes **may determine** community priorities such as:

- which regions or sectors to focus on next;
- which community projects to pursue;
- the direction of public communications and educational work.

Any such vote is marked **subject to legal and fiduciary approval**.

Finances above set thresholds and legal, fiduciary, safeguarding, privacy, employment, regulatory, or other non-delegable duties remain with responsible leadership and the fiscal sponsor. Leadership and sponsor keep the legal right to decline, pause, or reverse an outcome when required, with a documented reason and public record where lawful.

## Standing rules

- One person, one vote.
- No payment to vote and no donation weighting.
- No token, on-chain, or crypto mechanics.
- Eligibility, age rules, minimum participation periods, quorum, procedures, conflicts, appeals, duplicate-account controls, and emergency pauses must be defined before the first vote.
- Individual ballots remain private; proposals, methods, aggregate outcomes, pauses, and follow-up actions should be public.

The detailed draft is [POL-006](policies/POL-006-assembly-governance-and-voting.md).
