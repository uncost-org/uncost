# Governance

## Current accountability

Uncost is currently founder-led and pre-launch. It is recruiting advisers and an operating group covering nonprofit compliance, data methodology, AI and robotics, Shelter, Food, Energy, regional coordination, and community growth.

The movement's public identity is the movement rather than a founder personality, but the founder is not anonymous to legitimate due-diligence parties. Identity and relevant background may be disclosed confidentially to fiscal sponsors, banks, legal advisers, grantmakers, and other parties with a valid need.

## Intended structure

- **Fiscal sponsorship first.** Uncost intends to launch through a fiscal sponsor; its own nonprofit entity remains a longer-term option under review.
- **Founder accountability during formation.** Until formal governance exists, the founder is the responsible decision-maker and retains public-system credentials.
- **Advisory and operating group.** Named advisers or an interim oversight group should be added as they are recruited and consent to public identification.
- **Sector and region leads.** Volunteer coordination can expand across the fifteen sectors and local regions as capacity grows.
- **The Assembly.** Supporter proposals, surveys, and votes are intended to become increasingly meaningful, but legal, fiduciary, safeguarding, privacy, employment, regulatory, and sponsor duties remain with those legally accountable.
- **The Treasury.** Once funds are received, public reporting should show funds received, spending categories, reserves, sponsor/platform fees, and regular updates with appropriate privacy and security redactions.

## Decision boundary

No draft policy, roadmap target, issue, agent output, or community preference creates legal authority by itself. Adoption, delegation, spending, publishing, account changes, and regulated work require the appropriate human and institutional approval.
