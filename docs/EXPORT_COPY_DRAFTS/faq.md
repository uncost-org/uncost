## The basics

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source) — section heading

The basics

> DOSSIER-BACKED: July MOVEMENT_PLAN — "The snapshot" (nonprofit, nonpartisan movement to reduce the cost of living by directing AI, robotics, open-source tools ... toward real cost reduction; receipts first)

**What is Uncost, in one sentence?**

A nonprofit, nonpartisan movement to reduce the cost of living by directing AI, robotics, and open-source tools toward the essentials — starting with evidence, not promises.
⚠ DIVERGES FROM JULY SOURCE: July snapshot lists four levers — "AI, robotics, open-source tools, **and shared infrastructure**"; the export drops "shared infrastructure" — source wins

> DOSSIER-BACKED: July MOVEMENT_PLAN — "The snapshot" / Mission ("openly, lawfully, and nonpartisanly")

**Is this a political party or campaign?**

No. Uncost is strictly nonpartisan. We publish open data and public-interest analysis and welcome people across the political spectrum. We don’t back candidates, run campaigns, or do partisan lobbying.

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source) — reinforces route note "on-chain disavowal"; not stated in July MOVEMENT_PLAN

**Is this crypto, a token, or a DAO?**

No — deliberately. There are no tokens, no on-chain voting, and no crypto mechanics. The Assembly’s value is trust and participation, not financial speculation.

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source) — "against forced work / basic needs behind a paycheck" framing is not in the July MOVEMENT_PLAN or CONTROL

**Are you against work?**

No. Uncost is against *forced* work for survival. People should be free to work for creativity, contribution, family, learning, or purpose — but the basic needs of life shouldn’t stay locked behind a paycheck forever.

## Money & funding

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source) — section heading

Money & funding

> DOSSIER-BACKED: July MOVEMENT_PLAN — Participation ("Contribute: volunteer or, after the proper sponsor gate, donate"); CLAUDE.md boundary ("Donation launch waits for confirmed fiscal sponsorship")

**Can I donate right now?**

Not yet. We’re seeking fiscal sponsorship before accepting public tax-deductible donations. You can register an intention to give and we’ll email you the moment it opens — no money changes hands today.

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source) — "The Treasury" public financial record, internal receipt/approval trail; not named in July MOVEMENT_PLAN (which states only "transparent financial records when funding begins")

**How will you handle money transparently?**

Through The Treasury — a public financial record of funds received, spending categories, reserves, and fees, with redactions only where privacy or law requires. Every expense has an internal receipt and approval trail.

> DOSSIER-BACKED: July MOVEMENT_PLAN — Vision ("a world where money no longer determines access to life's essentials"); Assembly (bounded voice, no pay-to-vote implied)

**Do donations buy influence or votes?**

Never. There’s no payment to vote and no extra influence for donors — that’s a core principle, since the whole movement opposes wealth determining outcomes.

## The evidence

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source) — section heading

The evidence

> DOSSIER-BACKED: July MOVEMENT_PLAN — Trust posture ("receipts first, open corrections"); "The Case" (publish sources, dates, methods, confidence); CLAUDE.md ("Public facts require source/date/method/confidence")

**How do I know your numbers are real?**

Every figure carries a source, a date, and a confidence label. Anything hypothetical is marked “illustrative only.” The full method is in The Receipts, and corrections are logged publicly.
⚠ UNCITED: "The Receipts" is named as the location of "the full method" — no such method document is cited in the July source or CONTROL

> DOSSIER-BACKED: July MOVEMENT_PLAN — "How a cost gets uncosted" step 6 ("Track whether total cost actually falls, publishing negative results too")

**Won’t you just overpromise what AI can do?**

We use careful, task-specific language and publish uncertainty ranges. Where a model shows automation *doesn’t* beat the status quo once energy, capital, and maintenance are counted, we publish that negative result too.

> DOSSIER-BACKED: July MOVEMENT_PLAN — "The Case" ("All fifteen sectors stay visible; Shelter, Food, and Energy are the first deep focus")

**Why fifteen sectors?**

Because living isn’t one bill. We map all fifteen essentials of a decent life and go deep on three first — Shelter, Food, and Energy — to prove the model before expanding. See the Sectors.

## Voice & governance

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source) — section heading

Voice & governance

> DOSSIER-BACKED: July MOVEMENT_PLAN — "The Assembly" (structured recorded voice; "eventually formal votes where law and governance permit"; legal/fiduciary/safeguarding/sponsor duties remain with those accountable)

**What is the Assembly, and is my vote binding?**

The Assembly is how supporters propose, prioritise, and review the work, with transparent records. It is **currently advisory**. As the legal structure matures, votes may carry more weight where the law allows — but decisions with legal, fiduciary, or safeguarding duty always rest with responsible leadership and the fiscal sponsor. The exact binding-versus-advisory wording is being finalised and will be published in the governance policy.

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source) — "founder-led, recruiting advisory/operating group; founder identity disclosed confidentially for due diligence" is not in the July MOVEMENT_PLAN or CONTROL

**Who runs Uncost?**

It’s currently founder-led and recruiting an advisory and operating group. The public identity is the movement itself, but it isn’t anonymous to those who need to know: founder identity and background are disclosed confidentially for legitimate due diligence.

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source) — data-handling / "never sell data / never use pledge for partisan targeting" and the Privacy Policy (POL-001) reference are not in the July MOVEMENT_PLAN

**How is my data handled?**

Minimally. We use your details only for what you asked for, never sell data, and never use your pledge for partisan targeting. Full detail in the Privacy Policy (POL-001).
