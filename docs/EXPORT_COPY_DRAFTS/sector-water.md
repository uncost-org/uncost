## Worked example

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source) — the 6-step spine derives from MOVEMENT_PLAN.md "How a cost gets uncosted" (Measure / Publish / Break down / Match / Package / Track), but all Water-specific detail below is not in the dossier or the July source.

03 · How water gets uncosted

The worked example: cutting a district's water losses.

1. Measure the cost pressure — Regional data shows what a household pays for water and sanitation — and how much of that cost is treatment, pumping energy, and losses from leaks.
⚠ UNCITED: that regional data shows household water/sanitation cost broken into treatment, pumping energy, and leak losses — no source; no such data is registered in the dossier (which states no quantitative claim exists yet).

2. Publish the sources — Sources and assumptions are posted openly so anyone can check them.

3. Break down the cost — The cost is split into treatment, energy, distribution, losses, and maintenance.
⚠ UNCITED: the specific five-way cost breakdown (treatment, energy, distribution, losses, maintenance) is an asserted cost stack with no source.

4. Match mechanisms to components — Networked sensors and AI leak detection, efficient filtration, and rain capture where the climate suits it — potentially cutting losses, energy, and emergency repairs.
⚠ UNCITED: that networked sensors + AI leak detection + filtration + rain capture would cut losses, energy, and emergency repairs — an unquantified efficacy claim with no source (hedged with "potentially").

5. Package the playbook — Uncost publishes the open model, sources, and an operations playbook. A water co-op, utility, or municipality runs it — not Uncost.

6. Track the result — The dashboard follows whether water costs actually fall. Where energy or infrastructure costs mean a measure does not pay off, that negative result is published too.
