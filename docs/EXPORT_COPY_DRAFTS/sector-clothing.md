## Worked example

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source) — the six steps are a Clothing-specific dramatization of the Movement Plan's generic "How a cost gets uncosted" 6-step method; the sector-specific specifics below are not in the dossier or the July source

03 · How clothing gets uncosted

The worked example: repair and reuse before replacement.

1. Measure the cost pressure
Regional data shows what households spend on clothing and shoes — and how much is fast replacement of items that could be repaired.
⚠ UNCITED: that "regional data shows what households spend on clothing and shoes" and that a measurable share is "fast replacement of items that could be repaired" — no figure, region, or source, and the dossier registers no quantitative claim yet.

2. Publish the sources
Sources and assumptions are posted openly so anyone can check them.

3. Break down the cost
The cost is split into materials, production, laundry, and premature replacement.
⚠ UNCITED: the asserted cost-stack decomposition into "materials, production, laundry, and premature replacement" — presented as fact with no source.

4. Match mechanisms to components
Automated sizing to cut returns, repair services, textile recycling, and shared laundry — extending lifespan and cutting waste.
⚠ UNCITED: that "automated sizing" cuts returns and that these mechanisms extend lifespan and cut waste — mechanism-effect claims with no evidence registered.

5. Package the playbook
Uncost publishes a playbook that repair co-ops, community laundries, and local makers can run.

6. Track the result
The dashboard follows whether clothing cost falls. Where recycling or repair does not pay off locally, that is published too.
