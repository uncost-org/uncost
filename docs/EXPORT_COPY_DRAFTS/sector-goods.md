## Worked example

> DOSSIER-BACKED: framework mirrors MOVEMENT_PLAN "How a cost gets uncosted" 6 steps (Measure / Publish / Break down / Match / Package / Track); the Goods-specific narrative and figures are design-authored on top of that scaffold.

03 · How goods gets uncosted

The worked example: a hundred neighbours, one drill.
⚠ UNCITED: "a hundred neighbours, one drill" — illustrative ratio with no source; dossier registers no quantitative claim.

**1 Measure the cost pressure** — Regional data shows what households spend on tools, appliances, and furniture — much of it used rarely.
⚠ UNCITED: "Regional data shows what households spend on tools, appliances, and furniture — much of it used rarely" — no source, no figure, no region; dossier Evidence status says no public quantitative claim is registered yet.

**2 Publish the sources** — Sources and assumptions are posted openly so anyone can check them.

**3 Break down the cost** — The cost is split into purchase, storage, maintenance, and the waste of near-idle ownership.
⚠ UNCITED: "the waste of near-idle ownership" — asserts near-idle ownership as fact with no source.

**4 Match mechanisms to components** — Tool libraries, repair systems, and shared-access software so neighbours share what they seldom need alone.

**5 Package the playbook** — Uncost publishes an open software-and-operations model — with liability, safety, and maintenance checklists — that communities run themselves.
⚠ UNCITED: an "open software-and-operations model … that communities run themselves" is described as a deliverable; nothing in the dossier or July source states this artifact exists — do not claim built/shipped.

**6 Track the result** — The dashboard follows whether household goods cost falls. Where logistics or liability make sharing impractical, that is published too.
