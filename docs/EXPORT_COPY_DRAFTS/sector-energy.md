## Worked example

> DOSSIER-BACKED (skeleton): derived from MOVEMENT_PLAN.md "How a cost gets uncosted" 6-step sequence (Measure / Publish / Break down / Match / Package / Track). The energy-specific details below are export elaborations not present in the dossier or July source.

03 · How energy gets uncosted

The worked example: a neighbourhood shared-solar microgrid.
⚠ UNCITED: the entire "neighbourhood shared-solar microgrid" is a hypothetical worked example with no cited source, region, or figures.

1. **Measure** the cost pressure — Regional data shows what a household pays for electricity and heat — and how much of that bill is generation, grid charges, fuel, and peak-demand pricing.
⚠ UNCITED: that a household electricity/heat bill decomposes into "generation, grid charges, fuel, and peak-demand pricing" is asserted with no source.

2. **Publish** the sources — Sources and assumptions are posted openly so anyone can check them.

3. **Break down** the cost — The cost is split into generation, storage, grid connection, fuel, and the premium paid at peak times.
⚠ UNCITED: the cost-stack components ("generation, storage, grid connection, fuel, and the premium paid at peak times") are asserted with no source.

4. **Match** mechanisms to components — Shared rooftop or community solar, battery storage, and AI load management that shifts demand away from peak — potentially cutting fuel exposure, peak charges, and waste.
⚠ UNCITED: that shared solar + battery storage + AI load management would cut "fuel exposure, peak charges, and waste" is asserted (hedged as "potentially") with no source or figures.

5. **Package** the playbook — Uncost publishes the open model, the sources, a project brief, and an operations playbook. Uncost does not run the microgrid; an energy co-op, community group, nonprofit, or public utility does.

6. **Track** the result — The dashboard follows whether energy costs actually fall where the model is applied. Where capital and storage costs or grid rules mean it does not beat the incumbent supply, that negative result is published too.
⚠ UNCITED: the premise that energy costs may or may not "beat the incumbent supply" is a scenario assertion with no cited data.
