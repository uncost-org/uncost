## Policy body — The plain-language version

> DOSSIER-BACKED: Policy statement ("collect only the minimum data required for a stated purpose; never sell or rent personal data or use it for partisan targeting; and provide working access and deletion routes") and Rule 1 (visible no-sale/no-partisan-targeting statement)

We only ask for what we need — mainly your email if you sign the Pledge or ask for updates. We use it for the thing you asked for, nothing else. We don't sell your data, and we never use your pledge for partisan targeting. You can unsubscribe or ask us to delete your data anytime.

## 1 · Who we are

> DOSSIER-BACKED: Owner ("a working privacy inbox and responsible owner are required before collection") and the draft banner ("Uncost is pre-launch; this draft does not represent an incorporated nonprofit, an accepted fiscal sponsor")

Uncost ("we", "us") is a nonprofit, nonpartisan movement to reduce the cost of living. This policy explains how we handle personal data across uncost.org and our community channels. Until fiscal sponsorship is confirmed, data is handled under this draft standard; the adopted version will name the responsible legal entity.

## 2 · What we collect

> DOSSIER-BACKED: Policy statement ("collect only the minimum data required for a stated purpose") and Prospective data inventory (data categories); Rule 4 (privacy-preserving analytics, publish aggregates)

We practise data minimisation — we collect as little as possible:

- **Email address** — when you sign the Pledge or subscribe to updates.
- **Optional details** — a name or message you choose to send us, or how you'd like to help.
- **Basic, privacy-respecting analytics** — aggregate page usage, without building advertising profiles.
- **Anti-manipulation signals** — the minimum needed to reduce duplicate accounts and automated abuse in the Assembly.

## 3 · How we use it

> DOSSIER-BACKED: Purpose (pledge signers, subscribers, donors, volunteers, community members) and Rules 2/5 (opt-in collection; legal/sponsor requirements)

- To send the updates or confirmations you asked for.
- To record and count Pledge signatures as public support.
- To reply to your messages and in-kind or partnership offers.
- To operate the advisory Assembly fairly (one person, one vote).

- To meet legal, accounting, and safeguarding obligations.

## 4 · What we never do

> DOSSIER-BACKED: Policy statement / Rule 3 ("no sale, rental, or trade; no partisan use") for the first three items; the on-chain item is design-authored disavowal

- We never sell, rent, or trade your personal data.
- We never use your pledge or contact details for partisan political targeting.
- We never require payment to vote or to take part.
- We don't use tokens, on-chain voting, or crypto mechanics that expose your identity or funds.
⚠ REPO-VERIFIABLE (policies/POL-006 + POL-004): no-tokens / no-on-chain-voting / no-crypto-mechanics disavowal (not present in POL-002 dossier or the July MOVEMENT_PLAN/CONTROL source; design-authored)

## 5 · Sharing & processors

> DOSSIER-BACKED: Rule 6 ("keep a private processor/security register; map processing locations, transfer mechanisms, subcontractors") and Rule 5 ("Legal/sponsor requirements control records they administer")

We share data only with service providers who help us operate (for example, email delivery and hosting), under contracts that limit them to processing on our instructions. Once fiscal sponsorship is confirmed, the sponsor may process donation data under its own compliant rules. We disclose data if the law requires it, and we'll say so where we're permitted to.

## 6 · Retention

> DOSSIER-BACKED: Rule 5 ("every category needs a purpose-tied retention and deletion rule before collection. Legal/sponsor requirements control records they administer")

We keep personal data only as long as needed for the purpose you gave it, or as the law requires. If you unsubscribe or ask for deletion, we remove your data from active systems promptly, keeping only what we're legally required to retain.

## 7 · Your rights

> DOSSIER-BACKED: Policy statement ("provide working access and deletion routes as required") and Adoption checklist ("consent and deletion flows")

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, export, or delete your data, to object to certain processing, and to withdraw consent. To exercise any of these, email us — see section 10. We won't make you jump through hoops.

## 8 · Security

> DOSSIER-BACKED: Policy statement (data minimisation) and Rule 7 (incident handling — implies technical/organisational measures)

We protect data with reasonable technical and organisational measures and keep collection minimal, so there's less to protect in the first place. No system is perfectly secure, but we treat your trust as the whole point of the movement.

## 9 · Changes to this policy

> DOSSIER-BACKED: versioned/status-controlled dossier frontmatter (version, status, review_status) and Rule 1/Rules ("legal or safeguarding duty" approvals sit with responsible leadership and fiscal sponsor); public change-log framing is design-authored

This policy is version-controlled. Its reference number (POL-001) stays fixed while the version increments, and every change is logged in the public record with a date and reason. Supporters can propose changes through the advisory Assembly; requirements carrying legal or safeguarding duty are approved by the responsible leadership and fiscal sponsor.
