## Worked example

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source) — Shelter-specific instantiation of MOVEMENT_PLAN "How a cost gets uncosted" (Measure / Publish / Break down / Match / Package / Track). The six-verb spine derives from the July source; every Shelter-specific detail below is authored and unsourced. This is the sector "worked example" archetype flagged in the brief.

03 · How shelter gets uncosted

The worked example: a modular dwelling on serviced land.

**1 · Measure the cost pressure** — Regional data shows what a household pays for housing — and how much of that cost is land, materials, on-site labor, financing, and permitting delay.
⚠ UNCITED: "Regional data shows what a household pays for housing" and the cost-stack split into land, materials, on-site labor, financing, and permitting delay (no register source; no figure — asserted as a worked example)

**2 · Publish the sources** — Sources and assumptions are posted openly so anyone can check them.

**3 · Break down the cost** — The cost is split into land, materials, labor, financing, and the time cost of permitting and inspection.
⚠ UNCITED: cost split into "land, materials, labor, financing, and the time cost of permitting and inspection" (illustrative cost stack, no source)

**4 · Match mechanisms to components** — Factory-built modular components and 3D-printed elements, robotic assembly support, and AI tools that speed permitting and code checks — potentially cutting on-site labor, waste, and schedule.
⚠ UNCITED: "potentially cutting on-site labor, waste, and schedule" via modular / 3D-printed / robotic assembly / AI permitting tools (mechanism-to-savings assertion with no source; hedged with "potentially" but still a factual claim)

**5 · Package the playbook** — Uncost publishes the open model, the sources, a project brief, and a build playbook. Uncost does not build the housing; a community land trust, nonprofit builder, co-op, or public agency runs it.
> (Aligns with MOVEMENT_PLAN "The Projects": "Uncost publishes and supports the evidence and tools; local operators decide and act.")

**6 · Track the result** — The dashboard follows whether delivered housing costs actually fall where the model is applied. Where land, financing, and code constraints mean modular does not beat conventional build, that negative result is published too.
⚠ UNCITED: "whether delivered housing costs actually fall where the model is applied" implies a tracking dashboard in operation; no such instrument is registered (aligns in principle with MOVEMENT_PLAN step 6 on publishing negative results)
