## Worked example

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source): the six-step worked example is not present in the SEC-013 dossier. It is a design elaboration of the generic six-step "How a cost gets uncosted" method in MOVEMENT_PLAN.md, applied to Safety. Each factual/operational assertion below lacks a citable Safety source.

03 · How safety gets uncosted

The worked example: faster community disaster response.

1. **Measure** the cost pressure
Regional data shows what communities lose to disasters and slow emergency response — in money and in harm.
⚠ UNCITED: "Regional data shows what communities lose to disasters and slow emergency response — in money and in harm." — asserts existing regional data with no SRC-### record; dossier registers no quantitative claim.

2. **Publish** the sources
Sources and assumptions are posted openly so anyone can check them.

3. **Break down** the cost
The cost is split into preparedness, response time, coordination, and recovery.
⚠ UNCITED: cost-stack decomposition "preparedness, response time, coordination, and recovery" — design-authored breakdown, no source.

4. **Match** mechanisms to components
AI and robotics for emergency coordination, logistics, disaster recovery, and prevention — strictly for resilience, never enforcement or surveillance.

5. **Package** the playbook
Uncost publishes a playbook that emergency services, community groups, and agencies can run.
⚠ UNCITED: implies a Safety playbook that "emergency services, community groups, and agencies can run" — no such deliverable exists; roadmap status is Dossier (scaffold). Also note CLAUDE.md gates safety-sensitive/physical work behind qualified authorities.

6. **Track** the result
The dashboard follows whether resilience improves and recovery cost falls. Where a measure does not help, that is published too.
⚠ UNCITED: "The dashboard follows whether resilience improves and recovery cost falls." — asserts a functioning Safety dashboard/measurement; none is registered.
