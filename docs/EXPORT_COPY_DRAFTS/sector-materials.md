Where automation could bite — and what stays visible.

**What it includes** — Raw materials, recycling, reuse, manufacturing inputs, and waste streams.

**The opportunity** — Sorting robots, circular manufacturing, material recovery, reusable parts, and 3D-printing inputs.
⚠ DIVERGES FROM JULY SOURCE: dossier Opportunity reads "Sorting robotics, circular manufacturing, recovery, reusable parts, and local production inputs." Export reframes "local production inputs" as "3D-printing inputs" — a narrowing not in the dossier or July source — source wins.

⚠ DIVERGES FROM JULY SOURCE: dossier Guardrail reads "Account for energy, contamination, transport, market demand, and disposal." ## Worked example

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source): the six-step spine mirrors MOVEMENT_PLAN "How a cost gets uncosted" (Measure / Publish / Break down / Match / Package / Track), but every Materials-specific detail below is authored elaboration not present in the dossier or July source.

03 · How materials gets uncosted

The worked example: recovering value from waste streams.

1. Measure the cost pressure — Regional data shows what materials and manufacturing inputs cost — and how much usable material is lost to waste.
⚠ UNCITED: asserts regional data exists showing materials/manufacturing input costs and usable material lost to waste — no source; dossier registers no quantitative claim.

2. Publish the sources — Sources and assumptions are posted openly so anyone can check them.
> DOSSIER-BACKED: derives from MOVEMENT_PLAN step 2 ("Publish sources, dates, methods, and confidence").

3. Break down the cost — The cost is split into raw inputs, processing, waste handling, and lost recoverable value.
⚠ UNCITED: the specific cost-stack decomposition (raw inputs, processing, waste handling, lost recoverable value) is asserted with no source.

4. Match mechanisms to components — Sorting robots, circular manufacturing, material recovery, and reusable parts feeding local production and 3D printing.
⚠ UNCITED: names specific mechanisms as applicable to Materials cost components with no evidence record; also note "local production and 3D printing" restates the Opportunity divergence flagged above.

5. Package the playbook — Uncost publishes the model and a playbook that recyclers, makers, and municipalities can run.
⚠ UNCITED: asserts a Materials model/playbook and named audiences (recyclers, makers, municipalities) — no such artifact exists in the dossier; guard against claiming shipped/adopted per CLAUDE.md.

6. Track the result — The dashboard follows whether input costs fall. Where energy or contamination make recovery uneconomic, that is published too.
⚠ UNCITED: asserts a dashboard tracking Materials input costs — no source; dossier has no figures. The negative-results duty derives from MOVEMENT_PLAN step 6.
