## Worked example

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source)
The six-step "worked example: cheaper shared local mobility" is a sector-specific dramatization of the generic MOVEMENT_PLAN "How a cost gets uncosted" method (Measure / Publish / Break down / Match / Package / Track). The step verbs match the July source; the Transportation-specific descriptions below are authored, not sourced, and every embedded factual assertion is an uncited claim.

03 · How transportation gets uncosted

The worked example: cheaper shared local mobility.

**1 · Measure the cost pressure**
Regional data shows what people spend to get around — and how much is vehicle ownership, fuel, and time lost to poor options.
⚠ UNCITED: that "regional data shows what people spend to get around" broken into vehicle ownership, fuel, and time lost — no source, and no Transportation figures are registered (per Evidence status).

**2 · Publish the sources**
Sources and assumptions are posted openly so anyone can check them.

**3 · Break down the cost**
The cost is split into vehicles, fuel, maintenance, infrastructure, and the cost of missing transit.
⚠ UNCITED: the specific cost-stack decomposition (vehicles, fuel, maintenance, infrastructure, cost of missing transit) is asserted with no source.

**4 · Match mechanisms to components**
Route optimization, shared fleets, and delivery robotics — with autonomous shuttles only where lawful and safe.
> DOSSIER-BACKED: SEC-008 Opportunity (mechanism list matches).

**5 · Package the playbook**
Uncost publishes the model and a playbook that transit agencies, co-ops, and communities can run.
⚠ UNCITED: naming specific operator types (transit agencies, co-ops, communities) as able to run an Uncost model/playbook — no such playbook or model exists as a registered artifact.

**6 · Track the result**
The dashboard follows whether mobility cost falls. Where safety, regulation, or capital mean a measure does not work, that is published too.
⚠ UNCITED: "The dashboard follows whether mobility cost falls" asserts an existing dashboard tracking Transportation mobility cost; MOVEMENT_PLAN describes the Human Essentials Dashboard as near-term ask work, not live tracking.
