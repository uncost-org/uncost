## Mechanism

> DOSSIER-BACKED: MOVEMENT_PLAN.md §How a cost gets uncosted (lines 31–38)

The mechanism
How a cost gets uncosted.

1 — Measure
Measure the current cost and its context, starting from public data.

2 — Publish
Publish the sources, dates, methods, and confidence labels — openly, so anyone can check the work.

3 — Break down
Split the cost stack into its real components: labor, energy, land, materials, waste, market structure, financing, fees, and policy.
⚠ UNCITED: the specific nine-component cost stack ("labor, energy, land, materials, waste, market structure, financing, fees, and policy") — the July source says only "Break down the cost stack"; this enumerated component list is a design elaboration with no cited source.

4 — Match
Match plausible AI, robotics, sharing, infrastructure, or public-interest mechanisms to specific components.

5 — Package
Package the result as open evidence, software, a brief, or a playbook local groups can use. Uncost publishes; local operators decide and act.

6 — Track
Track whether total cost actually falls — and publish negative results with the same prominence as wins.

Working principles
Three rules keep it honest.

Receipts first
Every public number, claim, and cost estimate is sourced, dated, confidence-labeled, and open to correction.

Open and reusable

Nonpartisan practical optimism

## Is / is not

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source) — consistent with CLAUDE.md boundaries and honest-status posture; exact bullets are not enumerated in the July source summary

Plainly
What Uncost is — and is not.

Uncost is
- A nonprofit movement to reduce the cost of living.
- A public-benefit technology and evidence platform.
- A community for supporters, donors, volunteers, researchers, builders, planners, and experts.
- A practical bridge from cost-of-living frustration to projects communities can run.

Uncost is not
- A political party or candidate campaign.
- A crypto, DAO, or token project.
- A promise that every physical constraint disappears or that everything becomes free.
- A replacement for local democratic control or qualified professional judgment.
