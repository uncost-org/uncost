## What supporters can do

> DOSSIER-BACKED: MOVEMENT_PLAN.md "The Assembly" — "proposals, surveys, prioritization, and eventually formal votes" (the participation loop below elaborates the four steps; the elaboration itself is design-authored)

The participation loop

Propose. Deliberate. Advise. On the record.

01 — Propose

Any eligible supporter can raise a proposal. Each gets a public ASM reference, a classification, and a conflict screen.

⚠ REPO-VERIFIABLE (policies/POL-006 rule 5 "Proposal lifecycle"): proposals get "a public ASM reference, a classification, and a conflict screen" (specific mechanics not in July source)

02 — Deliberate

Published discussion with a balanced record of the arguments for and against — rigorous disagreement is the point.

03 — Advise

Surveys and recorded advisory votes on priorities: which regions or sectors next, which projects, what the public work emphasizes.

04 — See the record

Method, eligibility, aggregate result, turnout, and the action taken — or the documented reason it was declined — all public.

## Standing rules

> DESIGN-AUTHORED (no source)
> Note: the third rule below is the on-chain disavowal sentence flagged in the route note.

Standing rules

Rules that don't bend.

One person, one vote.
Participation is free and never weighted by donations, purchases, assets, or status.

No payment to vote. Donations never buy influence.
The whole movement opposes wealth determining outcomes — its own governance can't work otherwise.

No tokens, no on-chain voting, no crypto mechanics.
The Assembly's value is trust and participation, not speculation.

Ballots private, records public.
Individual ballots stay private; proposals, methods, aggregate outcomes, and follow-up actions are published.

Eligibility comes with The Pledge.
Signing The Pledge plus a privacy-preserving duplicate check — no biometrics, no government ID assumed.

## The boundary

> DOSSIER-BACKED: MOVEMENT_PLAN.md "The Assembly" — "Legal, fiduciary, privacy, safeguarding, employment, regulatory, and sponsor duties remain with those accountable." Also CONTROL.md/CLAUDE.md boundaries: outcomes advisory, binding authority requires legal structure that does not yet exist.

The honest boundary

What the Assembly can — and cannot — decide.

Advisory at launch

Every Assembly outcome starts advisory. Where law and governance allow, outcomes may come to determine bounded community priorities — which regions or sectors to focus on next, which community projects to pursue, the direction of public communications — and any such vote is marked subject to legal and fiduciary approval.

Reserved matters

Finances above set thresholds and legal, fiduciary, safeguarding, privacy, employment, and regulatory duties remain with responsible leadership and the fiscal sponsor. Any binding authority would require legal structure that does not yet exist. Eligibility mechanics, quorum, appeals, and pauses are defined in the governance policy before the first vote.

Read the draft governance policy (POL-006) — public review draft, not adopted.
