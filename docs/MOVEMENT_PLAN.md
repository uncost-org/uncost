# Movement plan — public repository summary

This is a concise public derivative of the final external Movement Plan and Roadmap. The source receipt and authority boundary are in [CONTROL.md](CONTROL.md).

## The snapshot

Uncost is a nonprofit, nonpartisan movement to significantly reduce the cost of living by directing AI, robotics, open-source tools, and shared infrastructure toward real cost reduction.

- **North Star:** make basic living needs less dependent on money.
- **Mission:** reduce the cost of basic living needs through evidence, open-source tools, community action, and practical automation paths—openly, lawfully, and nonpartisanly.
- **Vision:** a world where money no longer determines access to life's essentials.
- **Flagship:** the regional, source-led Human Essentials Dashboard.
- **Trust posture:** receipts first, open corrections, transparent financial records when funding begins, and a bounded supporter voice through The Assembly.

## The Movement

Reach people with visual, sourced explanations; bring them to a trusted front door; and offer clear paths to support, contribute, or partner. Public content must lead back to evidence and practical action rather than outrage alone.

## The Case

Define what one human needs, measure what it costs by region, explain cost drivers, identify plausible reduction mechanisms, and publish open methodology. All fifteen sectors stay visible; Shelter, Food, and Energy are the first deep focus.

## The Projects

Translate evidence into open software, data tools, planning models, kits, studies, and playbooks that communities and qualified partners can use. Uncost publishes and supports the evidence and tools; local operators decide and act.

## The Assembly

Give supporters a structured, recorded voice through proposals, surveys, prioritization, and eventually formal votes where law and governance permit. Legal, fiduciary, privacy, safeguarding, employment, regulatory, and sponsor duties remain with those accountable.

## How a cost gets uncosted

1. **Measure** the current cost and context.
2. **Publish** sources, dates, methods, and confidence.
3. **Break down** the cost stack.
4. **Match** plausible AI, robotics, sharing, infrastructure, or public-interest mechanisms to specific components.
5. **Package** the result as open evidence, software, a brief, or a playbook local groups can use.
6. **Track** whether total cost actually falls, publishing negative results too.

## Participation

- **Support:** free actions such as joining updates or sharing sourced material.
- **Contribute:** volunteer or, after the proper sponsor gate, donate.
- **Partner:** advise, build, coordinate, provide public data, or offer reviewed in-kind support.

## Current ask

The near-term work is the source library, first receipts-complete cost visual, and a multi-region Human Essentials Dashboard—plus fiscal-sponsor introductions, advisers, early contributors, and in-kind partners. These are directions, not open commitments or authorization to contact parties.
