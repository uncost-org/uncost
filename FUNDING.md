# Funding and fiscal sponsorship

Uncost is seeking fiscal sponsorship before accepting public tax-deductible donations. A path to an independent nonprofit entity remains a longer-term option under review.

## Current boundary

- There is no authorized public donation launch in this repository.
- No donation should be represented as tax-deductible until a fiscal sponsor is confirmed and the sponsor's actual receipt and guidance say so.
- Donated services, compute, model access, equipment, and volunteer time follow the sponsor's rules and must not be assumed to have an automatic tax-deductible cash value.
- Donations and in-kind support never buy conclusions, coverage, governance influence, data access, or preferential treatment.

## Intended funding mix

- grassroots donations after sponsorship;
- creator-led support under reviewed agreements;
- optional supporter items;
- grants and public-interest funding;
- project sponsorships with independence protections;
- in-kind compute, model access, software, research, expertise, equipment, logistics, and future site support.

## Transparency

Once funding begins, The Treasury should publish funds received, spending categories, project reserves, sponsor and platform fees, and regular updates, with privacy, security, payroll, donor, and vendor redactions where appropriate. Every expense retains an internal receipt and approval trail even where public reporting is aggregated.

Draft controls are in [POL-004](policies/POL-004-financial-controls-and-donations.md), [POL-005](policies/POL-005-conflict-of-interest.md), and [POL-010](policies/POL-010-in-kind-gift-acceptance.md).
