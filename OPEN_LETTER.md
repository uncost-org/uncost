# An open letter: uncost the cost of living

Living should not have a price tag.

People watch rent, groceries, energy, healthcare, insurance, and basic goods take a larger share of life while technology becomes more capable every year. AI and robotics can already reduce repetitive labor in parts of farming, construction, logistics, energy, maintenance, manufacturing, and care support. But lower production effort does not automatically become lower living costs.

Uncost exists to close that gap deliberately.

We will begin with the real world: what a dignified life requires, what those needs cost by region, what drives those costs, and which mechanisms can reduce them. Every public number should have a source and date. Every estimate should be labeled. Every proposed intervention should count capital, energy, materials, maintenance, safety, regulation, and local conditions—not just the attractive part of the model.

We are not against work. We are against forced work for survival. People should be free to work for creativity, ambition, contribution, family, learning, art, science, service, community, or purpose. The essentials of life should become less dependent on a paycheck as technology and shared infrastructure improve.

This is practical work, not a promise that costs vanish overnight. Land, materials, energy, maintenance, safety, skills, law, and governance remain real. Some proposed uses of automation will fail. When they do, we should publish the negative result and learn from it.

The first job is modest and measurable: build the source library; define regional baskets; publish the first receipts-complete cost visual; develop the Human Essentials Dashboard; and make the tools and methods reusable by communities, co-ops, nonprofits, public agencies, researchers, and builders.

Uncost is nonprofit in purpose, nonpartisan in operation, open where rights allow, and accountable to evidence. It seeks fiscal sponsorship before accepting public tax-deductible donations. It will earn trust through sources, corrections, transparent records, and steady delivery—not hype.

There is a place for supporters, researchers, designers, engineers, translators, planners, source reviewers, advisers, funders, and local partners.

**Living should not have a price tag. Join us in uncosting the cost of living.**
