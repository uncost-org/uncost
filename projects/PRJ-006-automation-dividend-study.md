# PRJ-006 — Automation Dividend Study

## Snapshot
- **Project:** Automation Dividend Study.
- **Role in the system:** the movement's macro evidence base — the "Why Now" section's claims, done properly. Where PRJ-003 models specific costs, this study documents the system-level pattern: productivity gains exist; do essential costs fall?
- **Status:** Draft; not started. Year 2/3 research direction.
- **Roadmap:** Year 2/3 research direction after receipts infrastructure exists. POL-001 and external review are hard gates.

## Sectors
Cross-sector by nature, anchored in the focus sectors: Shelter, Food, Energy first; Transportation and Goods second. It does not attempt all fifteen in the first report.

## The problem and the cost being reduced
The plan's central empirical claim — technology keeps advancing while essential costs don't fall accordingly — is currently supported by pointers to The Receipts. This study is the receipt: a sourced, reviewed analysis of where automation gains flow (prices, wages, margins, ownership) sector by sector, and — the constructive half — documented conditions under which gains demonstrably *did* reach prices (solar's cost curve being the canonical positive case). The cost reduced is the movement's dependence on borrowed or anecdotal macro claims.

## What it is / what it is not
It is a methodology-first, pre-registered research product: questions and methods published *before* results, so the conclusions can't be accused of working backward from the thesis. It is not advocacy for any redistribution policy, not a lobbying document, not partisan — and if the evidence complicates the movement's thesis, that gets published too (the negative-results commitment applies to the flagship narrative as much as to any scenario).

## MVP deliverables
1. **Pre-registered methodology:** questions, data sources, analytical approach, and review process, published before analysis begins.
2. **First report** covering 2–3 focus sectors: productivity trends, price pass-through, wage effects, margin/ownership concentration, with every figure SRC- referenced and confidence-labeled.
3. **Positive-case chapter:** where automation gains verifiably reached consumer prices, and what conditions made that happen — this chapter is what keeps the study constructive rather than purely critical.
4. **Review panel:** at least two external reviewers with differing economic perspectives, named in the report, sign-off before publication.
5. Plain-language public summary + full technical appendix.

**Explicitly cut from report one:** policy recommendations (education-only framing until POL-001 legal review says otherwise, and even then cautious), all-fifteen-sector coverage, forecasting.

## AI role and human gates
AI performs literature review, data extraction, and claim-source tracking; humans own methodology, interpretation, and every published conclusion (POL-007). The political-activity review (POL-001 rule 5) applies to the whole report, not just excerpts.

## Success metrics
Methodology published before analysis; both reviewers signed; every claim SRC- traceable; the report survives its first hostile external critique without a factual correction (interpretive disputes are fine and expected); at least one citation by an external researcher or journalist within 6 months.

## Resource posture

No budget is approved. Resourcing requires a separate plan, evidence, and founder approval after the applicable policy, sponsor, methodology, safety, legal, and partner gates. Historical estimates are not authorization.

## Timeline (phase-gated)
Gate 1: methodology pre-registered and legally reviewed against POL-001 → Gate 2: data collection complete → Gate 3: draft + red team → Gate 4: external review → publish. Rough effort: 10–16 weeks once started; correctly a Year-2/3 item after the Case trio establishes credibility.

## Public summary
The Automation Dividend Study asks a simple question with published methods and sources: when AI and robotics make things cheaper to produce, do the essentials of life get cheaper to buy — and under what conditions has that actually happened?
