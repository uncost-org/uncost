# PRJ-005 — Library of Things Kit

## Snapshot
- **Project:** Library of Things Kit (canonical name; "Pilot Kit" retained as alias).
- **Role in the system:** the movement's first fully community-runnable playbook — the access-over-idle-ownership thesis (the plan's drill example) turned into something a real group can launch. Its economics feed PRJ-004's tool-library module.
- **Status:** Draft; not started. Year 2 direction.
- **Roadmap:** Year 2 direction, with operator and legal review before publication or pilot use. Dates are movable.

## Sectors
- **Primary:** Goods. **Secondary:** Materials (repair), Leisure (recreation equipment libraries as a later template).

## The problem and the cost being reduced
Households individually own expensive, rarely used equipment; groups that try to fix this with a lending library stall on the unglamorous parts — liability, insurance, waivers, damaged-item disputes, maintenance schedules, and governance. The kit reduces the setup cost and, more importantly, the *risk* cost of getting those parts wrong.

## What it is / what it is not
It is a documentation and templates product — the plan's own wording: "including liability, safety, governance, and maintenance checklists." It is not inventory, not funding, not insurance itself, not legal advice (templates carry "adapt with local counsel" flags), and Uncost does not operate the libraries.

## MVP deliverables — liability first, because that's where these projects die
1. **Risk & liability guide:** waiver templates, insurance types and typical requirements by jurisdiction class, age restrictions for tool categories, incident-response template, and an explicit list of items the kit recommends *never* lending without professional-grade controls (e.g., high-risk power equipment).
2. **Safety pack:** per-category safety checklists, condition-inspection flows, product-recall monitoring routine, maintenance schedules.
3. **Governance pack:** membership models, borrowing rules, damaged/late/lost handling, dispute process, volunteer roles, simple treasurer template.
4. **Operations pack:** open-source inventory tooling recommendations, labeling, storage requirements honestly stated (storage is the hidden cost — POL-010 logic applied at community scale), launch checklist.
5. **Economics worksheet:** realistic setup and running costs vs. modeled member savings, ranges only — feeds PRJ-004.
6. One pilot partnership with an existing library-of-things (learn from operators before publishing; do not pretend to invent the field — OSS communities and existing LoT networks already know most of this).

**Explicitly cut:** custom software builds, physical space provision, any lending category requiring certifications at MVP.

## AI role and human gates
AI drafts templates from existing-operator research, compiles jurisdiction variations, and monitors recall feeds for the safety pack. Humans — including at least one experienced operator and, for the liability guide, counsel review — approve everything before publication (POL-007; legal-review flag on deliverable 1).

## Success metrics (MVP)
Kit reviewed by ≥2 experienced library-of-things operators; liability guide reviewed by counsel; one community launches or upgrades using the kit within 6 months of publication; zero safety incidents attributable to kit guidance (tracked via the incident-response template's reporting path).

## Resource posture

## Risk level and policy dependencies
- **Risk level:** medium-high for a documentation project — physical-harm liability attaches to bad safety guidance even secondhand.
- **Safety review required:** yes — legal review of the liability guide is a hard gate; POL-008 applies if any pilot community involves vulnerable groups.
- **Policy dependencies:** POL-008 (conditional), POL-009 (licensing), POL-007 (review gates), POL-010 (the storage/cost-to-accept logic reused at community scale).

## Public summary
The Library of Things Kit is a free, open starter pack for communities that want a shared tool and equipment library — covering the parts that usually kill these projects: liability, insurance, safety checklists, governance, and honest economics.
