# POL-008 — Safeguarding & Pilot Safety Policy (P1)

**Owner:** Founder until a named safeguarding lead exists (naming one is an adoption requirement for any pilot). **Applies to:** any activity involving vulnerable people, physical-world operations, field research, or sensitive data collection.

## Purpose
Uncost's plan touches high-safety sectors (Healthcare, Care, child-facing Education) as *studies only*. This policy is the mechanism that keeps that promise true under enthusiasm and growth pressure.

## Policy statement
No Uncost activity involving minors, vulnerable adults, direct services, or physical pilots proceeds without a written safety plan, a named accountable owner, and — where applicable — sponsor and legal review. Studies study; they do not quietly become services.

## Rules
1. **Hard gates (from the Plan):** no child-facing work of any kind before privacy, curriculum, bias, guardian-consent, and human-oversight safeguards are in place and legally reviewed; Healthcare and Care remain research-only; Safety-sector work is community-resilience and disaster-response framing only — never enforcement or surveillance.
2. **Covered activities:** anything involving minors, unhoused people, migrants, disabled people, patients, disaster-affected or low-income communities; any physical pilot in any sector; interviews, filming, or data collection in sensitive contexts.
3. **Minimum written safety plan:** purpose and expected benefit; participant group and vulnerability assessment; local legal/regulatory requirements; consent and withdrawal process; data/privacy plan (POL-002); physical safety plan; safeguarding contact and incident path; partner due diligence; insurance and sponsor/entity review; explicit stop/pause criteria.
4. **Conduct rules:** never promise services Uncost cannot provide; never pressure anyone to share trauma or hardship stories; incentives must not be coercive; images, recordings, names, and quotes need specific consent; background checks where work touches minors or vulnerable adults, per jurisdiction.
5. **Stop triggers:** credible risk of harm, unsafe partner behavior, consent breakdown, privacy breach, legal concern, harassment, any medical/safety incident, or press/social escalation — pause first, assess second.
6. **Incidents:** private, factual, immediate reporting to the safeguarding owner and sponsor; documented resolution before restart.

## Adoption checklist
Name the safeguarding lead; build the safety-plan template; define "vulnerable participant" and background-check requirements per launch jurisdiction; legal review before the first physical pilot of any size.
