# POL-008 — Safeguarding & Pilot Safety Policy (P1)

**Owner:** a named accountable safeguarding lead is required before any covered activity. **Applies to:** vulnerable people, physical operations/pilots, field research, and sensitive-context data collection.

## Purpose

Keep Healthcare, Care, child-facing Education, Safety, and other high-risk work within the final plan's research and safety boundaries.

## Policy statement

No covered activity proceeds without a named lead, written risk/safety plan, consent/privacy controls, accountable qualified partners, and all applicable sponsor, legal, permit, site, insurance, and professional gates. Studies do not quietly become services.

## Rules

1. **Sector limits:** Healthcare and Care remain research-only; child-facing Education requires reviewed privacy, curriculum, bias, guardian-consent, safeguarding, and human oversight; Safety is resilience/disaster response only, never enforcement or surveillance.
2. **Covered activity:** work involving minors, vulnerable adults, unhoused people, migrants, disabled people, patients, disaster-affected or low-income communities; physical pilots; sensitive interviews, filming, or data collection.
3. **Physical-work gates:** no physical pilot or demonstration commitment without sufficient funding, a qualified local operator, accountable partner, operational expertise, permits and site permission, insurance, and qualified legal/safety review.
4. **Written plan:** purpose/benefit; participants and vulnerability; law/permits; consent/withdrawal; privacy/data; physical safety; safeguarding contact; partner diligence; insurance; stop/pause criteria; incident and restart authority.
5. **Conduct:** no service promises Uncost cannot meet; no pressure to share hardship; no coercive incentives; specific consent for names/images/quotes; required background checks according to jurisdiction and role.
6. **Stop triggers:** credible harm risk, unsafe partner conduct, consent failure, privacy breach, legal concern, harassment, medical/safety incident, or unsafe escalation means pause first and assess second.
7. **Incidents:** report immediately to the named lead and responsible partner/sponsor; preserve private factual records; do not restart until documented clearance.

## Adoption checklist

Named safeguarding lead; definitions and jurisdiction checks; safety-plan template; qualified operator/partner; funding; permits/site permission; insurance; background-check rules; sponsor/legal/safeguarding review.

**Private records:** participant data, background checks, risk plans, partner diligence, locations/security detail, and incidents. Publish only safe summaries.
