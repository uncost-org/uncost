# POL-006 — Assembly Governance & Voting Policy (P0)

**Owner:** Founder, with mandatory legal and fiscal-sponsor review before any formal vote runs. **Applies to:** all Assembly proposals, discussions, surveys, votes, and records.

## Purpose

Turn the final plan's Assembly direction into cautious, runnable rules: a genuine supporter voice, bounded by law and fiduciary duty, with no wealth influence and no crypto mechanics.

## Current status

The Assembly is not operational. This draft creates no delegated authority. No formal vote runs until this policy is independently reviewed, legally/sponsor reviewed where applicable, formally adopted, and the required privacy and integrity controls exist.

## Direction

Supporters may propose ideas, prioritize work, raise objections, and vote on priorities and proposed changes. Wherever law and governance allow, Assembly outcomes **may determine** community priorities such as which regions or sectors to focus on next, which projects to pursue, and the direction of public communications and educational work. Every such vote is marked **subject to legal and fiduciary approval**.

Leadership and the fiscal sponsor retain the legal right to decline, pause, reverse, or limit an outcome when required by legal, fiduciary, safeguarding, privacy, employment, regulatory, contractual, or sponsor duty. The reason and resulting action are recorded publicly where lawful.

## Rules

### 1. Eligibility and weight

One person, one vote. No payment to vote; donations buy no extra weight or influence. Eligibility requires signing The Pledge plus a privacy-preserving duplicate/automation control. Age/guardian rules, any minimum participation period, and the personhood method require POL-002 and legal review before operation. No biometric or government-ID requirement is assumed by this draft.

### 2. Decision classification

Before discussion opens, each proposal is classified:

- **Potentially determinative, subject to legal and fiduciary approval:** community priorities, sector/region sequencing, community projects, and public educational direction where delegation is lawful.
- **Advisory:** finance above sponsor-set thresholds; legal, fiduciary, safeguarding, privacy, employment, regulatory, contractual, or sponsor-controlled matters.
- **Out of scope:** candidate/party campaigning, illegal activity, weakening non-waivable safeguards, individual personnel or private-data decisions, or matters Uncost has no authority to decide.

### 3. Proposal lifecycle

`ASM-###` assignment → classification and conflict screen → published discussion window → balanced pro/con record → published voting method and eligibility snapshot → voting window → aggregate result and turnout → leadership/sponsor review where required → implementation decision or documented decline/pause/reversal → public change record.

Minimum discussion/voting windows, quorum, methods, appeals, recusal, duplicate controls, and emergency pause rules remain adoption decisions. They are not fixed by this draft.

### 4. Ballot and integrity principles

Voters may change a ballot before close if the adopted system supports it. Pro/con material must be visible and attributable. Any comprehension material is educational, not a pass/fail voting test. Binding decisions use legible methods established before the vote; allocation exercises are advisory unless the adopted policy expressly provides otherwise.

No tokens, on-chain voting, or crypto mechanics. Individual ballots remain private. Proposal text, classification, method, aggregate results, pauses, reasons, and follow-up actions are public records where lawful.

## Adoption checklist

Independent Claude review; founder review; legal and fiscal-sponsor review of delegation and reserved duties; POL-002 review of eligibility/privacy; POL-005 conflict process; adopted quorum, participation, ballot, appeal, pause, and age rules; tested public record/correction flow; moderator and incident procedures.
