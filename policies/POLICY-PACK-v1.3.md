# Uncost Policy Pack v1.3

> **Not legal advice — not yet in force.** These are working drafts prepared with AI assistance. Under the final plan's policy and safeguard requirements, every policy here requires real human review, and the P0 policies require review by qualified counsel (nonprofit / election law / data protection) before adoption or operational use. Draft publication is not adoption. No policy in this pack is in effect until formally adopted and versioned.

## 0. How this pack works

**Alignment.** This pack is grounded in the **final external Movement Plan and Roadmap (2026-07-15)**: fifteen sectors with canonical short names (final-control registry), the four parts (The Movement, The Case, The Projects, The Assembly), receipts-first, movement-first public identity, and the decided entity path — **fiscal sponsorship first, own nonprofit entity as a longer-term option under review**. All DAO, token, on-chain, stablecoin, crypto-treasury, and "entity path undecided" language from v0.1 is removed: Uncost is not a crypto, DAO, or token project, and its policies must never read as if it might be.

**Sponsor precedence.** Once a fiscal sponsor is confirmed, the sponsor's policies control wherever they conflict with this pack. Each policy below operates as Uncost's internal standard *within* the sponsor's framework, and each must be re-reviewed against the sponsor's requirements at signing.

**Adoption model.** Each policy carries: an ID (public prefix scheme), priority, owner, review cadence, and who it applies to. Until a board or steward group exists, the founder is owner and approver of record; the fiscal sponsor becomes co-approver for policies carrying legal or fiduciary weight. The Assembly may propose and comment on policy changes, and may vote on them, but requirements carrying legal, fiduciary, or safeguarding duty are approved by responsible leadership and the sponsor and cannot be weakened by popular vote (Plan, "Policies and safeguards").

**Public/private split.** Publish each adopted policy in full on the website and in the public repo with its POL- reference number (Plan requirement). Keep private: incident logs, disclosure registers, vendor lists, specific thresholds where publication would invite gaming, and personal data in reports.

**Applies to (default, all policies).** The founder; advisers and any future stewards/board; staff and contractors; volunteers, moderators, sector leads and region leads; automated agents acting for Uncost; and vendors/partners where their contracts incorporate these policies. Called "covered people" below.

# POL-001 — Political Activity Policy (P0)

**Owner:** Founder (co-approved by fiscal sponsor once confirmed). **Applies to:** all covered people and all official Uncost channels.

## Purpose
Protect Uncost's nonpartisan, charitable public-benefit posture — a condition of fiscal sponsorship, grant eligibility, and the movement's cross-partisan credibility — while preserving evidence-based public education.

## Policy statement
Uncost is strictly nonpartisan. It educates, publishes evidence, and builds tools; it does not support or oppose candidates, parties, or campaigns, and it treats its nonpartisan posture as non-negotiable infrastructure rather than a tone preference.

## Rules
1. **Permitted:** nonpartisan research, dashboards, sector dossiers, explainers, and public education; source-led analysis of policies, systems, incentives, and market structures; candidate-neutral civic education only if legally reviewed and evenly applied; coalition work across the political spectrum where mission-aligned and nonpartisan.
2. **Prohibited:** supporting or opposing any candidate, party, PAC, slate, or campaign committee; coordinating with any campaign on strategy, messaging, data, events, endorsements, or fundraising; using Uncost funds, lists, community spaces, dashboards, brand, or volunteer infrastructure for electioneering; "vote for/against" messaging; candidate scorecards or voter guides without prior legal review.
3. **Lobbying and ballot measures:** no lobbying or ballot-measure activity of any kind without written founder approval *and* fiscal-sponsor approval *and* legal review. Under a US fiscal sponsor, lobbying limits are governed by the sponsor's 501(c)(3) status; assume the answer is "no" until the sponsor says otherwise in writing. Where a cost driver is policy, Uncost's role is source-led education, open data, and nonpartisan public-interest analysis — never partisan lobbying or campaign work (Plan language, verbatim intent).
4. **Personal capacity:** covered people are free political actors personally, but must not imply Uncost endorsement, use Uncost resources or data, or blend Uncost branding with personal political activity. Sector and region leads must take particular care given their visible roles.
5. **Founder and spokesperson neutrality:** the founder, spokespeople, coordinators, agents, and official channels must not imply Uncost endorsement while acting in an Uncost role. Personal political activity must be clearly separate.
6. **Election-season review:** in the 90 days before a major election in any launch region, all public content referencing legislation, government programs, or officeholders gets a second human review against this policy before publication.
7. **Agents and creators:** automated agents and paid/creator partners are bound by rules 1–2 in all Uncost-branded output; creator agreements must incorporate this policy by reference.

## Escalation
Suspected violations go to the founder (or sponsor compliance contact) immediately; content is unpublished first and adjudicated second.

## Public disclaimer (publish with the policy)
"Uncost is nonpartisan. We critique systems, incentives, and policies with evidence; we do not endorse or oppose candidates or parties."

## Adoption checklist
Legal review of rules 2–3 against the chosen sponsor's rules; add the disclaimer to the website footer and press page; incorporate into creator and volunteer agreements.

# POL-002 — Privacy & Data Protection Policy (P0)

**Owner:** Founder as interim privacy owner, with a named privacy inbox. **Applies to:** all covered people, all systems, all regions Uncost collects data in.

## Purpose
Protect everyone whose personal data touches Uncost — pledge signers, subscribers, donors, volunteers, community members, and research participants — across multiple countries and legal regimes.

## Policy statement
Uncost collects the minimum personal data needed for a stated purpose, never sells or rents personal data, never uses it for partisan targeting, and gives people a working path to access and deletion. Because Uncost operates internationally from day one, it designs to the stricter common denominator of applicable regimes (GDPR-style consent, purpose limitation, and deletion rights) rather than to any single country's floor.

## Data inventory (maintain as a living register)
The Pledge signature and email with mailing-list opt-in status; newsletter preferences; website and dashboard analytics; community identities and moderation logs (Discord/GitHub/Assembly); donation records (held primarily by the sponsor/payment platform); volunteer and lead applications; any future opt-in community submissions; AI tool inputs/outputs containing personal data.

## Rules
1. **The Pledge page commitments are binding** (the final plan's Pledge requirements): explicit opt-in consent; a visible "We will not sell your data or use your pledge for partisan campaign targeting" statement; an age/guardian rule; notable-signer public listing only with written consent, with the exact wording shown to them and consent retained on file; a working retention/deletion request mechanism; a linked privacy notice.
2. **Collection:** opt-in only for email, stories, quotes, images, and recordings; no sensitive-category data unless necessary, approved, and specially protected; no data collection from minors or vulnerable people without a POL-008 safeguarding review first.
3. **Use limits:** no sale, rental, or trade of personal data; no partisan or campaign use, ever; no export of community or donor data into third-party AI tools unless approved, minimized, and covered by adequate vendor terms; no publication of identifiable stories without written, revocable consent.
4. **Analytics and dashboards:** privacy-preserving analytics preferred; publish aggregates by default; suppress small cell sizes that could identify individuals; all dashboard figures follow The Receipts labeling (source, date, confidence).
5. **Retention:** emails until unsubscribe plus a suppression record; donation records per sponsor/legal requirements; moderation logs only as long as needed for safety and accountability; everything else gets a purpose-tied retention line in the register.
6. **Vendors:** only vendors with adequate security, deletion/export paths, and written data-processing terms; maintain a private processor list with regions of storage.
7. **Incidents:** on discovering a material privacy incident — within 72 hours: preserve evidence, contain access, identify affected data and people, notify the founder and sponsor, and determine legal and user notification duties with counsel.
8. **Cross-border note:** before collecting data across countries, map processing locations, applicable law, transfer mechanisms, and user rights; the privacy notice must state where data is processed.

## Adoption checklist
Require explicit Pledge opt-in; publish the no-sale/no-partisan-targeting statement; define age/guardian handling; record notable-signer consent; implement deletion and retention controls; draft the public privacy notice for uncost.org; choose the analytics stack and cookie posture; stand up the privacy inbox and deletion workflow; legal review with specific attention to the multi-country pledge list.

# POL-003 — Code of Conduct (P0)

**Owner:** Founder, delegated day-to-day to moderators. **Applies to:** all Uncost spaces — community platforms, GitHub, Assembly discussions and votes, events, calls, and official social threads.

## Purpose
Keep Uncost's spaces safe enough for hard, evidence-driven argument — including from beginners and skeptics — without tolerating abuse.

## Policy statement
Uncost welcomes rigorous disagreement about systems, evidence, costs, and tradeoffs. It does not tolerate harassment, hate, threats, doxxing, or manipulation, and it holds its own moderators and leaders to the same standard as everyone else.

## Rules
1. **Expected:** argue ideas, not people; cite evidence where possible; assume good faith until behavior shows otherwise; respect names, boundaries, privacy, and accessibility needs; make room for non-experts; disclose conflicts when recommending vendors, creators, tools, or partners (see POL-005).
2. **Prohibited:** harassment, stalking, threats, intimidation, or sustained disruption; hate, slurs, or dehumanization based on protected characteristics; doxxing or publishing private information; sexual harassment; spam, scams, impersonation, brigading, or platform manipulation; incitement to violence or illegal activity; partisan campaigning in Uncost spaces (POL-001); misuse of member, donor, or volunteer data.
3. **High-sensitivity spaces:** any channels touching care, safety, or personal hardship run with proactive moderation, slow-mode where useful, pinned crisis-resource links, and an explicit "this is not professional advice or a substitute for professional support" notice.
4. **Leads and moderators** are covered people with elevated visibility; violations by them escalate directly to the founder and carry faster consequences, not slower.

## Enforcement ladder
Reminder → warning → content removal → temporary mute/restriction → temporary suspension → permanent ban/contributor removal → platform, legal, or emergency escalation where safety requires. Moderators use judgment on severity; ladder steps can be skipped for threats, doxxing, or credible safety risk.

## Reporting and appeals
A private reporting channel with what/where/when, evidence if available, and the requested outcome; retaliation for good-faith reports is itself a violation. One appeal is available for restrictions and bans, except where the conduct involved threats, doxxing, credible safety risk, or legal exposure.

## Adoption checklist
Name and train at least two human moderators before community launch; publish a short public version plus this full text; add CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md to the rebuilt repo; write the moderator playbook (private).

# POL-004 — Financial Controls & Donation Policy (P0)

**Owner:** Founder as interim treasurer, superseded by sponsor finance controls at signing. **Applies to:** every dollar and every in-kind resource Uncost receives or spends.

## Purpose
Protect donor trust and the movement's independence; make The Treasury's "every dollar has a receipt" promise operationally true; prevent misuse in a founder-led structure with no staff yet.

## Policy statement
Uncost funds are used only for mission-aligned, documented, approved purposes. Donations never purchase editorial control, favorable coverage, governance influence, data access, or preferential treatment — the same firewall that governs company partnerships (Plan, "Partnering with cost-reducing companies").

## Rules
1. **Acceptance:** accept donations that are legal, mission-aligned, and free of improper conditions. Escalate or refuse gifts that are illegal or sanctioned; anonymous above a steward-set threshold; conditioned on partisan activity, content outcomes, endorsement, vendor selection, data access, or governance influence; reputationally high-risk; or restricted in ways Uncost cannot honor.
2. **Restricted gifts** require written terms, a named owner, a budget category, a reporting expectation, and an agreed exit/return rule if the restriction becomes impossible or mission-inconsistent.
3. **Approval thresholds (interim placeholders; sponsor thresholds control at signing):** up to $250 — founder or operations lead; $251–$1,000 — two approvals, one being the founder; over $1,000 — founder approval plus written rationale retained; recurring commitments — reviewed before acceptance regardless of size; related-party spend — POL-005 review and recusal required at any amount.
4. **The single-founder problem, named honestly:** with one person, "dual control" is aspirational. Until an adviser or steward exists, the compensating controls are: the fiscal sponsor as second approver for material spend, a complete receipt-and-rationale trail for everything, monthly Treasury publication, and no cash handling. The founder may not approve their own reimbursements; those route to the sponsor.
5. **Reimbursements:** receipt, business purpose, date, amount, and approver required; submitted within 30 days; no self-approval.
6. **Banking:** segregated accounts under the sponsor's structure only; no cryptocurrency, tokens, or on-chain treasury of any kind (Plan: The Assembly and The Treasury use no crypto mechanics).
7. **The Treasury (public record):** publish funds received, spending categories, project reserves, sponsor and platform fees, and regular updates — with privacy, security, payroll, donor, and vendor redactions where appropriate. Every expense keeps an internal receipt and approval trail even where public reporting is aggregated (Plan language). Publication cadence: monthly once donations open; quarterly summaries at minimum.

## Escalation
Any suspected misuse: freeze the relevant spend, notify the sponsor, document, and adjudicate before resuming.

## Adoption checklist
Do not open a public donation flow until sponsorship is confirmed. Confirm sponsor thresholds and receipting; choose the donation platform through the sponsor; set the anonymous-gift threshold; stand up the Treasury publication template; legal/sponsor review of rules 3–4.

# POL-005 — Conflict of Interest Policy (P0)

**Owner:** Founder; register maintained privately; sponsor sees it on request. **Applies to:** founder, advisers/stewards, moderators, sector and region leads, contractors, agents acting for Uncost, and anyone with spending, moderation, content, or governance authority.

## Purpose
Protect Uncost from hidden influence — and protect the founder from the appearance of it, which for a movement-first, founder-led organization is nearly as damaging as the real thing.

## Policy statement
Covered people disclose interests that could reasonably affect their Uncost judgment before participating in the affected decision. Disclosure is routine and safe; concealment is the violation.

## Rules
1. **Disclose:** paid relationships with vendors, creators, sponsors, donors, or partners; equity, referral, affiliate, or compensation interests tied to decisions; close personal or family relationships with vendors or beneficiaries; donor attempts to influence research, governance, hiring, or content; creator deals that could bias recommendations.
2. **Founder related-party rule (standing, explicit):** the founder operates separate commercial ventures. Any Uncost use of, payment to, or data exchange with a founder-connected entity requires a written basis (license or agreement), disclosure in the register, arm's-length terms with documented comparables, and — once a sponsor exists — sponsor sign-off. For any founder-connected data or services, any cost or source data used by Uncost requires a written license/permission basis, related-party disclosure, and strict separation of commercial and charitable datasets; the nonprofit must not subsidize or blur private commercial assets into charitable outputs.
3. **Process:** disclose before the decision → record in the private register → a non-conflicted reviewer (sponsor, adviser, or designated steward) decides mitigation → the conflicted person recuses from the vote/approval where the interest is material → related-party transactions carry written rationale and comparable alternatives.
4. **Gifts:** no covered person accepts personal gifts, travel, payments, or perks above a modest threshold (set at adoption; interim: US$50) from any vendor, donor, or partner without disclosure and approval.
5. **Annual attestation:** every covered person confirms their disclosures annually and at role changes.

## Adoption checklist
Create the disclosure form and register; set the gift threshold; run the founder's first full disclosure (including all commercial entities) before sponsor due diligence, so it is a strength in that conversation rather than a discovery.

# POL-006 — Assembly Governance & Voting Policy (P0)

**Owner:** Founder, with mandatory legal and fiscal-sponsor review before any formal vote runs. **Applies to:** all Assembly proposals, discussions, surveys, votes, and records.

## Purpose

Turn the final plan's Assembly direction into cautious, runnable rules: a genuine supporter voice, bounded by law and fiduciary duty, with no wealth influence and no crypto mechanics.

## Current status

The Assembly is not operational. This draft creates no delegated authority. No formal vote runs until this policy is independently reviewed, legally/sponsor reviewed where applicable, formally adopted, and the required privacy and integrity controls exist.

## Direction

Supporters may propose ideas, prioritize work, raise objections, and vote on priorities and proposed changes. Wherever law and governance allow, Assembly outcomes **may determine** community priorities such as which regions or sectors to focus on next, which projects to pursue, and the direction of public communications and educational work. Every such vote is marked **subject to legal and fiduciary approval**.

Leadership and the fiscal sponsor retain the legal right to decline, pause, reverse, or limit an outcome when required by legal, fiduciary, safeguarding, privacy, employment, regulatory, contractual, or sponsor duty. The reason and resulting action are recorded publicly where lawful.

## Rules

### 1. Eligibility and weight

One person, one vote. No payment to vote; donations buy no extra weight or influence. Eligibility requires signing The Pledge plus a privacy-preserving duplicate/automation control. Age/guardian rules, any minimum participation period, and the personhood method require POL-002 and legal review before operation. No biometric or government-ID requirement is assumed by this draft.

### 2. Decision classification

Before discussion opens, each proposal is classified:

- **Potentially determinative, subject to legal and fiduciary approval:** community priorities, sector/region sequencing, community projects, and public educational direction where delegation is lawful.
- **Advisory:** finance above sponsor-set thresholds; legal, fiduciary, safeguarding, privacy, employment, regulatory, contractual, or sponsor-controlled matters.
- **Out of scope:** candidate/party campaigning, illegal activity, weakening non-waivable safeguards, individual personnel or private-data decisions, or matters Uncost has no authority to decide.

### 3. Proposal lifecycle

`ASM-###` assignment → classification and conflict screen → published discussion window → balanced pro/con record → published voting method and eligibility snapshot → voting window → aggregate result and turnout → leadership/sponsor review where required → implementation decision or documented decline/pause/reversal → public change record.

Minimum discussion/voting windows, quorum, methods, appeals, recusal, duplicate controls, and emergency pause rules remain adoption decisions. They are not fixed by this draft.

### 4. Ballot and integrity principles

Voters may change a ballot before close if the adopted system supports it. Pro/con material must be visible and attributable. Any comprehension material is educational, not a pass/fail voting test. Binding decisions use legible methods established before the vote; allocation exercises are advisory unless the adopted policy expressly provides otherwise.

No tokens, on-chain voting, or crypto mechanics. Individual ballots remain private. Proposal text, classification, method, aggregate results, pauses, reasons, and follow-up actions are public records where lawful.

## Adoption checklist

Independent Claude review; founder review; legal and fiscal-sponsor review of delegation and reserved duties; POL-002 review of eligibility/privacy; POL-005 conflict process; adopted quorum, participation, ballot, appeal, pause, and age rules; tested public record/correction flow; moderator and incident procedures.

# POL-007 — Responsible AI Policy (P1)

**Owner:** Founder. **Applies to:** all AI use in Uncost content, dashboards, models, voiceover, research, agents, and operations.

## Purpose
Uncost is an AI-forward movement making public claims about AI; its own AI use must be exemplary, transparent, and human-accountable — its credibility depends on practicing what it publishes.

## Policy statement
AI may research, draft, summarize, design, model, and operate; humans remain accountable for every public claim, every dollar, every safety decision, every personal-data action, and every enforcement action.

## Rules
1. **Human review is mandatory before:** publishing any public content, statistic, dashboard figure, or sector claim; sending official email or social posts; generating synthetic voice or likeness; any action affecting donors, volunteers, minors, or vulnerable people; spending funds, changing permissions, or making legal commitments.
2. **Receipts-first applies to AI output:** every factual public claim carries a source (SRC- ID), date, and confidence label; estimates, scenarios, and derived figures are labeled as such; inventing sources, statistics, quotes, endorsements, or lived-experience stories is prohibited and treated as a serious violation.
3. **Disclosure:** disclose AI use where material to trust — generated imagery/video/voice, substantially AI-written reports, AI-built dashboards or models, and any automated agent interacting with the public identifies itself as automated.
4. **Content guardrails for agents** (final-plan content guardrails): no partisan language; no investment or financial advice; no medical, legal, or child-safety claims; no privacy-invasive collection; no overpromising AI/robotics capability — task-specific language and uncertainty ranges only.
5. **Data limits:** no private community, donor, or pilot data into third-party AI tools without approval, minimization, and adequate vendor terms (POL-002); no training or fine-tuning on community data without explicit consent.
6. **Synthetic media:** no deceptive synthetic media, period; voice/likeness only with consent; clearly labeled where used (including founder voiceover if synthetic tools assist it).
7. **Agent autonomy line:** agents may draft, research, and prepare; agents may not independently publish, post, spend, approve, moderate high-stakes cases, contact vulnerable people, or mutate public systems. Publishing credentials stay under human control. Any future delegation requires a separate, narrow, revocable, logged founder approval and does not follow from this draft.
8. **Incidents:** an AI-caused public error triggers correction-with-changelog on the affected surface, a register entry, and a review of the failing gate.

## Adoption checklist
Approve the tool/vendor list; add the AI-disclosure note to the content workflow; build the pre-publication claim-check checklist; wire the SRC- register into the dashboard pipeline.

# POL-008 — Safeguarding & Pilot Safety Policy (P1)

**Owner:** Founder until a named safeguarding lead exists (naming one is an adoption requirement for any pilot). **Applies to:** any activity involving vulnerable people, physical-world operations, field research, or sensitive data collection.

## Purpose
Uncost's plan touches high-safety sectors (Healthcare, Care, child-facing Education) as *studies only*. This policy is the mechanism that keeps that promise true under enthusiasm and growth pressure.

## Policy statement
No Uncost activity involving minors, vulnerable adults, direct services, or physical pilots proceeds without a written safety plan, a named accountable owner, and — where applicable — sponsor and legal review. Studies study; they do not quietly become services.

## Rules
1. **Hard gates (from the Plan):** no child-facing work of any kind before privacy, curriculum, bias, guardian-consent, and human-oversight safeguards are in place and legally reviewed; Healthcare and Care remain research-only; Safety-sector work is community-resilience and disaster-response framing only — never enforcement or surveillance.
2. **Covered activities:** anything involving minors, unhoused people, migrants, disabled people, patients, disaster-affected or low-income communities; any physical pilot in any sector; interviews, filming, or data collection in sensitive contexts.
3. **Minimum written safety plan:** purpose and expected benefit; participant group and vulnerability assessment; local legal/regulatory requirements; consent and withdrawal process; data/privacy plan (POL-002); physical safety plan; safeguarding contact and incident path; partner due diligence; insurance and sponsor/entity review; explicit stop/pause criteria.
4. **Conduct rules:** never promise services Uncost cannot provide; never pressure anyone to share trauma or hardship stories; incentives must not be coercive; images, recordings, names, and quotes need specific consent; background checks where work touches minors or vulnerable adults, per jurisdiction.
5. **Stop triggers:** credible risk of harm, unsafe partner behavior, consent breakdown, privacy breach, legal concern, harassment, any medical/safety incident, or press/social escalation — pause first, assess second.
6. **Incidents:** private, factual, immediate reporting to the safeguarding owner and sponsor; documented resolution before restart.

## Adoption checklist
Name the safeguarding lead; build the safety-plan template; define "vulnerable participant" and background-check requirements per launch jurisdiction; legal review before the first physical pilot of any size.

# POL-009 — Open-Source Licensing Policy (P1)

**Owner:** Founder. **Applies to:** all public repos, published content, datasets, brand assets, and inbound contributions.

## Purpose
Open by default, precise about what "open" means: code, content, data, brand, and personal stories carry different rights, and conflating them creates legal exposure and community disputes.

## Rules
1. **Code:** Apache-2.0 is the current repository default (permissive plus explicit patent grant). Any change requires founder and legal review plus a written repository rationale.
2. **Documentation and educational content:** CC BY 4.0 is the current repository default, except where third-party material, personal stories, brand material, or partner restrictions apply.
3. **Original datasets:** CC BY 4.0 or ODC-BY for datasets Uncost fully owns; third-party data always retains its source license, attribution, and usage limits — the SRC- register records the license of every source, and the dashboards must not republish data whose terms forbid it.
4. **Brand:** the Uncost name, logo, wordmark, slogans, and campaign identity are **not** open-licensed; a brand-use page governs permitted use. Open tools, protected identity.
5. **Personal stories and community contributions of a personal nature are never open by default**; they require specific, scoped, revocable consent (POL-002).
6. **Inbound contributions:** DCO sign-off (or CLA if the sponsor requires) before substantial contributions; contributors confirm they hold the rights to what they submit; AI-assisted contributions disclosed where material and reviewed for license contamination.
7. **Repo hygiene:** every public repo carries LICENSE, README, CONTRIBUTING.md, CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md (POL-003), and SECURITY.md with a private disclosure contact and a response-window commitment.

## Adoption checklist
Confirm Apache-2.0 + CC BY 4.0 defaults at repo rebuild; create the four repo template files; publish the brand-use page; record every existing source's license in the SRC- register before dashboard launch.

# POL-010 — In-Kind Gift Acceptance Policy (P1)

**Owner:** Founder, with sponsor sign-off on receipting. **Applies to:** all non-cash support — compute, model access, software credits, equipment, materials, storage, logistics, data access, and future demonstration sites.

## Purpose
In-kind support is first-class in Uncost's funding model (Plan) — and the most common source of hidden strings, valuation trouble, and dead-weight cost. This policy makes acceptance deliberate.

## Rules
1. **Acceptance criteria — accept only when all four hold:** mission fit (it advances a registered project or operation); usability (Uncost can actually deploy it within a reasonable window); net benefit (the full cost to accept — storage, transport, insurance, maintenance, disposal — is materially below the value received); and clean terms (no editorial, governance, data, or recognition strings; the POL-004 firewall applies identically to in-kind).
2. **Cost-to-accept is assessed in writing** for physical goods before acceptance: where it will be stored, who transports it, who insures and maintains it, and what disposal costs if unused. Storage and logistics capacity are themselves valued in-kind contributions (Plan), and their absence is a legitimate reason to decline equipment.
3. **Valuation and tax:** the donor is responsible for any valuation or appraisal; Uncost and its sponsor issue receipts per the sponsor's rules; no Uncost material ever states or implies that an in-kind gift, donated service, or volunteer time carries an automatic tax-deductible cash value (Plan language). Creator content donations follow the same rule — no deductibility representations.
4. **Compute, API credits, and model access:** record expiry dates, acceptable-use terms, and any data-handling constraints in the register; credits with terms that conflict with POL-002 or POL-007 are declined; concentration risk is reviewed annually (no single provider dependency without a fallback note).
5. **Restricted in-kind** (usable only for a named project) follows POL-004's restricted-gift rules.
6. **Disclosure:** in-kind gifts appear in The Treasury by category and, for material gifts, by named source unless the donor requests anonymity and leadership plus sponsor agree it carries no influence risk; any in-kind relationship with a company featured in a case study is disclosed in that case study (Plan firewall).
7. **Refusal and exit:** Uncost may decline or return any gift; unused physical gifts are returned, transferred to an aligned nonprofit, or disposed of with a register entry.

## Adoption checklist
Sponsor review of receipting; create the in-kind register (item, source, value basis, terms, expiry, location, disposition); confirm storage capacity honestly before the first equipment acceptance.
