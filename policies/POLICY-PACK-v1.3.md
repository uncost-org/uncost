# POL-001 — Political Activity Policy (P0)

**Owner:** Founder, with fiscal-sponsor co-approval once a sponsor is confirmed. **Applies to:** all covered people and official Uncost channels.

## Purpose

Protect Uncost's nonpartisan public-benefit posture, future sponsor compliance, grant eligibility, and cross-partisan credibility while preserving evidence-based public education.

## Policy statement

Uncost is strictly nonpartisan. It educates, publishes evidence, and builds tools; it does not support or oppose candidates, parties, campaigns, PACs, or slates.

## Rules

1. **Permitted:** source-led research, dashboards, sector dossiers, explainers, public education, and analysis of policies, systems, incentives, and market structures; candidate-neutral civic education only after qualified legal review and even application.
2. **Prohibited:** electioneering; campaign coordination; endorsements; campaign fundraising; partisan use of Uncost funds, lists, data, community spaces, tools, brand, or volunteer infrastructure; and candidate scorecards or voter guides without qualified legal review.
3. **Lobbying and ballot measures:** no lobbying or ballot-measure activity may be undertaken in Uncost's name unless the founder and any confirmed fiscal sponsor approve it in writing after qualified legal review under the sponsor's rules and applicable law. Until then, policy-facing work is limited to source-led education, open data, and nonpartisan public-interest analysis. Partisan lobbying and campaign work are prohibited.
4. **Personal capacity:** covered people remain free political actors personally, but must not imply Uncost endorsement, use Uncost resources or data, or blend Uncost branding with personal political activity.
5. **Founder, spokesperson, coordinator, agent, and creator neutrality:** anyone acting in an Uncost role follows this policy; creator agreements incorporate it where relevant.
6. **Election-period review:** launch regions and review periods remain TBD after qualified legal review. Until adopted, public material referring to candidates, campaigns, ballot measures, or officeholders requires a second human review before publication.

## Escalation

On a suspected breach, pause or unpublish the affected material first, preserve evidence, and escalate to the founder and any confirmed sponsor compliance contact. Investigations and legal advice remain private.

## Public disclaimer

> Uncost is nonpartisan. We critique systems, incentives, and policies with evidence; we do not endorse or oppose candidates or parties.

## Adoption checklist

Qualified election-law review; confirmed sponsor rules where applicable; election-period definitions; website/press disclaimer; creator and volunteer terms; private incident process.

# POL-002 — Privacy & Data Protection Policy (P0)

**Owner:** Founder as interim privacy owner; a working privacy inbox and responsible owner are required before collection. **Applies to:** all personal-data handling by Uncost.

## Purpose

Protect pledge signers, subscribers, donors, volunteers, community members, research participants, and anyone else whose personal data may touch Uncost.

## Policy statement

Uncost is pre-launch and may be accessed by people in more than one jurisdiction. Before collecting personal data, it must map the intended collection, processing, storage, transfers, vendors, and applicable law; collect only the minimum data required for a stated purpose; never sell or rent personal data or use it for partisan targeting; and provide working access and deletion routes as required.

## Prospective data inventory

Before any collection opens, maintain a private inventory of each proposed data category, purpose, lawful/consent basis, vendor, storage region, access role, retention period, deletion route, transfer mechanism, and applicable user rights. References to Pledge, newsletter, analytics, community, donation, volunteer, moderation, research, or AI records are prospective and do not imply those systems are live.

## Rules

1. **Requirements before opening any Pledge or data-collection flow:** explicit opt-in consent; a visible no-sale/no-partisan-targeting statement; reviewed age/guardian handling; notable-signer listing only with written scoped consent; a working access/deletion mechanism; and a linked privacy notice.
2. **Collection:** opt-in where required for email, stories, quotes, images, and recordings; no sensitive data unless necessary, approved, and specially protected; no collection involving minors or vulnerable people without POL-008 review.
3. **Use limits:** no sale, rental, or trade; no partisan use; no identifiable-story publication without specific revocable consent; no transfer to AI or other vendors without approved need, minimization, security, deletion/export paths, and adequate written terms.
4. **Analytics and dashboards:** prefer privacy-preserving analytics; publish aggregates; suppress small cells that could identify people; apply source/date/confidence receipts to figures.
5. **Retention:** every category needs a purpose-tied retention and deletion rule before collection. Legal/sponsor requirements control records they administer.
6. **Vendors and transfers:** keep a private processor/security register; map processing locations, transfer mechanisms, subcontractors, and user rights before cross-border processing.
7. **Incidents:** preserve evidence, contain access, identify affected data and people, notify the responsible owner and any confirmed sponsor without delay, and determine legal/user notice deadlines with qualified counsel. Do not assume one universal notification period.

## Adoption checklist

Data/vendor/transfer map; privacy notice; working privacy inbox; consent and deletion flows; age/guardian rule; retention schedule; processor terms; analytics/cookie posture; qualified privacy/legal review.

**Private records:** personal data, processor/security detail, requests, moderation evidence, and incident files.

# POL-003 — Code of Conduct (P0)

**Owner:** Founder; moderation may be delegated only after named moderators are trained. **Applies upon adoption to:** any future Uncost repository, community, Assembly, event, call, or official social space.

## Purpose

Create spaces safe enough for rigorous, evidence-led disagreement, including participation by beginners and skeptics, without tolerating abuse.

## Policy statement

Uncost welcomes disagreement about systems, evidence, costs, and tradeoffs. It does not tolerate harassment, hate, threats, doxxing, retaliation, or manipulation, and holds leaders and moderators to the same standard as everyone else.

## Rules

1. **Expected:** argue ideas rather than people; cite evidence where possible; respect names, boundaries, privacy, and accessibility needs; make room for non-experts; disclose conflicts when recommending vendors, tools, creators, or partners.
2. **Prohibited:** harassment, stalking, threats, intimidation, hate, slurs, doxxing, sexual harassment, spam, scams, impersonation, brigading, manipulation, incitement, partisan campaigning in Uncost spaces, or misuse of private data.
3. **High-sensitivity spaces:** before opening any space involving care, safety, or personal hardship, provide proactive moderation, suitable pacing controls, reviewed support notices, and a clear statement that Uncost is not a substitute for professional support.
4. **Leaders and moderators:** elevated visibility means faster escalation, not special protection.

## Enforcement

Proportionate measures may include reminder, warning, removal, restriction, suspension, contributor removal, ban, platform escalation, or legal/emergency escalation. Serious threats, doxxing, or credible safety risks may skip steps.

## Reporting and appeals

A private reporting route must exist before public community opening. Good-faith reporters are protected from retaliation. A bounded appeal is available where safety/legal exposure does not make it inappropriate. Reports, evidence, moderator notes, appeals, and safety records remain private.

## Adoption checklist

Name and train at least two human moderators; establish reporting/escalation ownership; publish reporting and appeal routes; prepare a private moderation playbook; review jurisdiction-appropriate crisis resources. No public community or Assembly opens before these gates pass.

# POL-004 — Financial Controls & Donation Policy (P0)

**Owner:** Founder before sponsorship; any confirmed fiscal sponsor's controls supersede conflicting draft detail. **Applies upon adoption to:** every financial or in-kind resource Uncost receives or spends.

## Purpose

Protect donor trust and movement independence, make every financial action traceable, and prevent misuse in a founder-led pre-launch structure.

## Policy statement

No public donation flow opens before a fiscal sponsor or other lawful approved structure is confirmed. Funds and gifts may be used only for mission-aligned, documented, approved purposes. Support never buys editorial control, favorable coverage, ranking, inclusion, governance influence, data access, or preferential treatment.

## Rules

1. **Acceptance:** accept only legal, mission-aligned support with clean terms. Escalate or refuse sanctioned, improperly conditioned, influence-seeking, reputationally unsafe, or unadministrable gifts.
2. **Restricted gifts:** require written terms, named ownership, an approved category, reporting expectations, and an exit/return rule.
3. **No equity:** Uncost does not take equity or investment positions in companies it studies or features. Any financial or in-kind relationship with such a company is disclosed with the relevant public work.
4. **Thresholds:** approval, anonymous-gift, materiality, and recurring-commitment thresholds are TBD at adoption and must be reconciled with the confirmed sponsor's controls. No v1.2 placeholder amount is carried forward as an adopted rule. Related-party spend always requires POL-005 disclosure, independent review or recusal, and sponsor approval where applicable.
5. **No self-approval:** the founder may not approve their own reimbursement or related-party payment. Before a sponsor/independent approver exists, such spending remains held.
6. **Records:** every transaction retains purpose, date, amount/value basis, receipt or agreement, restriction, and approver. Reimbursement timing follows adopted sponsor controls.
7. **Banking:** only segregated accounts under the confirmed lawful structure; no cash handling and no cryptocurrency, token, on-chain, wallet, or multisig treasury.
8. **Public Treasury summaries:** once lawful flows open, publish safe aggregate funds, spending categories, reserves, and fees. Exact cadence and redactions are set at adoption with sponsor/privacy review.

## Escalation

Freeze suspected misuse, preserve records, notify the responsible independent/sponsor owner when available, and adjudicate before resuming.

## Adoption checklist

Confirmed sponsor/channel; approvers; thresholds; anonymous-gift rule; receipting; restricted-gift process; reporting cadence; POL-005/010 alignment; qualified legal/accounting/sponsor review.

**Private records:** banking, receipts, donor/vendor/payroll detail, investigations, and security-sensitive controls.

# POL-005 — Conflict of Interest Policy (P0)

**Owner:** Founder; a private register and independent review path are required before adoption. **Applies to:** anyone with spending, content, moderation, governance, data, partnership, or hiring authority.

## Purpose

Protect Uncost from hidden influence and from the appearance that private interests determine public-interest work.

## Policy statement

Covered people disclose interests that could reasonably affect their Uncost judgment before participating in an affected decision. Disclosure is routine; concealment is a breach.

## Rules

1. **Disclose:** financial, equity, referral, affiliate, compensation, donor, vendor, partner, creator, close personal/family, or other interests tied to the decision.
2. **Related parties:** any use of or payment to a founder- or leadership-connected entity, dataset, service, or asset requires a written permission, licence, or agreement; documented arm's-length comparables; charitable/commercial dataset and account separation; disclosure; independent review and recusal; and fiscal-sponsor approval where applicable.
3. **Process:** disclose before the decision; record it privately; assign a non-conflicted reviewer; document mitigation/comparables; recuse the conflicted person where material; retain the decision trail.
4. **Gifts:** the modest-gift threshold is set only at adoption with sponsor review. Until then, covered people accept no personal gift, travel, payment, or perk from a party seeking an Uncost decision without disclosure and independent written approval.
5. **Attestation:** every covered person confirms disclosures at onboarding, role change, and the adopted recurring interval.

## Adoption checklist

Independent reviewer; disclosure form/register; gift threshold; recusal and related-party procedure; first private attestation; sponsor/professional review.

**Private records:** complete disclosures, related-party registers, diligence, investigations, personal background, and non-public transaction details.

# POL-006 — Assembly Governance & Participation Policy (P0)

**Owner:** Founder; legal and fiscal-sponsor review is mandatory before any formal vote. **Applies upon adoption to:** Assembly proposals, discussions, surveys, votes, and records.

## Purpose

Give supporters genuine voice while preserving law, fiduciary duty, safeguarding, privacy, and accountable leadership.

## Current status

The Assembly is not operational. It is advisory at launch. This draft creates no delegated authority and fixes no eligibility, age, membership-duration, quorum, voting-window, appeal, or duplicate-prevention mechanics.

## Direction

Supporters may propose ideas, prioritize work, raise objections, and vote on priorities and proposed changes. Assembly outcomes may determine bounded community priorities only after a prior explicit written lawful delegation. Leadership and any confirmed fiscal sponsor retain duties that cannot lawfully or responsibly be delegated.

## Rules

1. **Eligibility and weight:** where a vote is used, it is one person, one vote. Participation is free and is never weighted by donations, purchases, assets, or status. Eligibility may use The Pledge plus a reviewed privacy-preserving duplicate/automation check. No biometric or government-ID requirement is assumed.
2. **Advisory by default:** at launch, every Assembly outcome is advisory.
3. **Delegated decisions:** a decision is non-advisory only after a prior written delegation identifies the domain, limits, duration, accountable owner, legal/sponsor basis, and reversal/pause duties. Every delegated-decision ballot and result must state **subject to legal and fiduciary approval**. That label alone does not create delegation.
4. **Reserved matters:** legal, fiduciary, financial, safeguarding, privacy, employment, regulatory, incident, personnel, contractual, and sponsor-controlled matters remain reserved unless lawful delegation is expressly documented; non-waivable duties remain reserved.
5. **Proposal lifecycle:** `ASM-###` assignment; classification and conflict screen; published discussion; balanced pro/con record; pre-published method/eligibility; voting window; aggregate result/turnout; accountable review; implementation or documented decline/pause/reversal; public change record where lawful.
6. **Mechanics remain TBD:** eligibility, age/guardian rules, minimum membership, quorum, windows, methods, recusal, appeals, duplicate prevention, accessibility, and emergency pause require privacy, legal, sponsor, and integrity review before adoption.
7. **Integrity:** no token, DAO, on-chain, conviction, quadratic, tradable-right, or wealth-weighted mechanics. Individual ballots and fraud investigations remain private; safe aggregate records are public.
8. **Opening gate:** at least two trained human moderators, POL-002 privacy/personhood review, incident procedures, and separate founder/legal/sponsor approval are required before public Assembly opening.

## Adoption checklist

Independent Claude review; founder review; legal/fiscal-sponsor review; written reserved-duty and delegation framework; POL-002/003/005 alignment; adopted mechanics; tested record/correction flow; two moderators; incident and appeal procedures.

# POL-007 — Responsible AI Policy (P1)

**Owner:** Founder. **Applies to:** AI use in Uncost research, drafting, content, dashboards, models, design, voice, agents, and operations.

## Purpose

Make Uncost's AI use transparent, source-led, privacy-respecting, and human-accountable.

## Policy statement

AI may assist with research, drafting, summarisation, design, and modelling, and may perform only approved, logged, low-risk internal automation. Humans remain accountable for every public claim, publication, financial action, personal-data action, safety decision, enforcement action, and external commitment.

## Rules

1. **Human review:** mandatory before public content/statistics, official messages, synthetic media, actions affecting donors/volunteers/vulnerable people, spending, permission changes, enforcement, or legal/external commitments.
2. **Receipts first:** factual public claims carry source ID, date, and confidence; estimates/scenarios/derived values are labeled; fabricated sources, statistics, quotes, endorsements, or lived-experience stories are prohibited.
3. **Disclosure:** disclose material AI use, including generated media, substantially AI-produced reports, models, dashboards, and any approved public-facing automation.
4. **Guardrails:** no partisan output; no financial, medical, legal, or child-safety advice; no invasive collection; no overclaiming AI/robotics capability.
5. **Data:** no private community, donor, participant, or pilot data in third-party AI without approved need, minimization, consent/legal basis where required, security, and vendor terms; no training on community data without explicit consent.
6. **Synthetic media:** no deception; voice or likeness requires consent and clear labeling.
7. **Autonomy:** AI may draft, research, and prepare. It may not independently publish, post, spend, approve, moderate high-stakes cases, contact vulnerable people, or mutate public/external systems. Credentials remain human-controlled; any future delegation is narrow, logged, revocable, and separately approved.
8. **Incidents:** correct public errors with a changelog, preserve an incident record, and repair the failed gate.

## Adoption checklist

Approved tool/vendor list; claim-check workflow; disclosure standard; security/privacy review; human approval roles; credential controls; incident process. This draft does not imply a public agent, production source pipeline, or approved vendor list exists.

**Private records:** restricted-data prompts, vendor/security assessments, incidents, access controls, and credential detail.

# POL-008 — Safeguarding & Pilot Safety Policy (P1)

**Owner:** a named accountable safeguarding lead is required before any covered activity. **Applies to:** vulnerable people, physical operations/pilots, field research, and sensitive-context data collection.

## Purpose

Keep Healthcare, Care, child-facing Education, Safety, and other high-risk work within the final plan's research and safety boundaries.

## Policy statement

No covered activity proceeds without a named lead, written risk/safety plan, consent/privacy controls, accountable qualified partners, and all applicable sponsor, legal, permit, site, insurance, and professional gates. Studies do not quietly become services.

## Rules

1. **Sector limits:** Healthcare and Care remain research-only; child-facing Education requires reviewed privacy, curriculum, bias, guardian-consent, safeguarding, and human oversight; Safety is resilience/disaster response only, never enforcement or surveillance.
2. **Covered activity:** work involving minors, vulnerable adults, unhoused people, migrants, disabled people, patients, disaster-affected or low-income communities; physical pilots; sensitive interviews, filming, or data collection.
3. **Physical-work gates:** no physical pilot or demonstration commitment without sufficient funding, a qualified local operator, accountable partner, operational expertise, permits and site permission, insurance, and qualified legal/safety review.
4. **Written plan:** purpose/benefit; participants and vulnerability; law/permits; consent/withdrawal; privacy/data; physical safety; safeguarding contact; partner diligence; insurance; stop/pause criteria; incident and restart authority.
5. **Conduct:** no service promises Uncost cannot meet; no pressure to share hardship; no coercive incentives; specific consent for names/images/quotes; required background checks according to jurisdiction and role.
6. **Stop triggers:** credible harm risk, unsafe partner conduct, consent failure, privacy breach, legal concern, harassment, medical/safety incident, or unsafe escalation means pause first and assess second.
7. **Incidents:** report immediately to the named lead and responsible partner/sponsor; preserve private factual records; do not restart until documented clearance.

## Adoption checklist

Named safeguarding lead; definitions and jurisdiction checks; safety-plan template; qualified operator/partner; funding; permits/site permission; insurance; background-check rules; sponsor/legal/safeguarding review.

**Private records:** participant data, background checks, risk plans, partner diligence, locations/security detail, and incidents. Publish only safe summaries.

# POL-009 — Open-Source Licensing Policy (P1)

**Owner:** Founder. **Applies to:** repositories, published content, datasets, brand assets, and inbound contributions.

## Purpose

Be precise about rights: code, educational content, data, brand, third-party material, and personal stories require different treatment.

## Rules

1. **This repository:** the root `LICENSE` applies Apache-2.0 to source code and CC BY 4.0 to original documentation/educational content, with exclusions stated in `NOTICE.md`. These are applied repository-specific licences, not automatic defaults for every future asset.
2. **Future code/content defaults:** Apache-2.0 for code and CC BY 4.0 for original educational content are proposed defaults pending IP/licensing and sponsor review before reuse elsewhere. Every repository/file states the licence actually applied; silence grants no licence.
3. **Datasets:** CC BY 4.0 or ODC-BY are proposed for datasets Uncost fully owns, pending review. Third-party data retains its source licence, attribution, and restrictions; prohibited republication remains prohibited.
4. **Brand:** the Uncost name, logo, wordmark, slogans, and campaign identity are not open-licensed by the repository licences. Separate brand-use terms are required.
5. **Personal material:** stories and personal community contributions are never open by default and require specific, scoped, revocable consent.
6. **Inbound contributions:** contributors confirm rights and disclose material AI assistance. DCO versus CLA remains an adoption/sponsor decision; neither is implied live by this draft.
7. **Repository hygiene:** state actual licences; retain source/attribution/terms; provide README, contribution, conduct, and private security-reporting routes appropriate to the repository.

## Adoption checklist

Qualified IP/licensing and sponsor review; decide future code/content/data defaults; DCO or CLA; brand-use terms; source-licence register; security response commitment.

**Private records:** vulnerability reports, security details, personal consents, and restricted source terms.

# POL-010 — In-Kind Gift Acceptance Policy (P1)

**Owner:** Founder; any confirmed sponsor controls receipting. **Applies upon adoption to:** proposed non-cash support such as compute, model/software access, credits, equipment, materials, storage, logistics, data access, and sites.

## Purpose

Accept useful mission-aligned support without hidden strings, valuation errors, privacy/licensing problems, or dead-weight costs.

## Rules

1. **Four acceptance tests:** mission fit; practical usability; net benefit after storage/transport/insurance/maintenance/disposal; and clean terms with no editorial, governance, data, ranking, recognition, or influence strings.
2. **Physical goods:** document total cost, storage, transport, insurance, maintenance, permissions, safety, and disposition before acceptance.
3. **Valuation and tax:** the donor is responsible for valuation/appraisal; any receipt follows confirmed sponsor rules; Uncost never claims automatic tax deductibility for services, volunteer time, access, or other in-kind support.
4. **Compute, credits, and model access:** record expiry, permitted use, data handling, concentration, and fallback; decline terms conflicting with POL-002/007/009.
5. **Data or data access:** require documented provenance, permission or licence, permitted-use terms, retention/deletion rules, and compatibility with POL-002, POL-007, and POL-009.
6. **Restricted in-kind:** follows POL-004's restricted-gift controls.
7. **Disclosure and anonymity:** safe Treasury summaries disclose category and material source where required. Permitted anonymity applies only to general reporting and may not conceal a conflict or financial/in-kind relationship with a company Uncost studies or features.
8. **Refusal and exit:** Uncost may decline or return support; disposition of unused goods follows written terms and a retained record.

## Adoption checklist

Confirmed sponsor receipting; acceptance authority; materiality rule; private gift register; storage/logistics; concentration fallback; data/access review; disclosure process.

This draft does not imply equipment, credits, data access, storage, or a sponsor receipting process is active.

**Private records:** detailed gift register, contracts, location/security detail, value basis, and donor information lawfully withheld.
