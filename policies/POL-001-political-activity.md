# POL-001 — Political Activity Policy (P0)

**Owner:** Founder (co-approved by fiscal sponsor once confirmed). **Applies to:** all covered people and all official Uncost channels.

## Purpose
Protect Uncost's nonpartisan, charitable public-benefit posture — a condition of fiscal sponsorship, grant eligibility, and the movement's cross-partisan credibility — while preserving evidence-based public education.

## Policy statement
Uncost is strictly nonpartisan. It educates, publishes evidence, and builds tools; it does not support or oppose candidates, parties, or campaigns, and it treats its nonpartisan posture as non-negotiable infrastructure rather than a tone preference.

## Rules
1. **Permitted:** nonpartisan research, dashboards, sector dossiers, explainers, and public education; source-led analysis of policies, systems, incentives, and market structures; candidate-neutral civic education only if legally reviewed and evenly applied; coalition work across the political spectrum where mission-aligned and nonpartisan.
2. **Prohibited:** supporting or opposing any candidate, party, PAC, slate, or campaign committee; coordinating with any campaign on strategy, messaging, data, events, endorsements, or fundraising; using Uncost funds, lists, community spaces, dashboards, brand, or volunteer infrastructure for electioneering; "vote for/against" messaging; candidate scorecards or voter guides without prior legal review.
3. **Lobbying and ballot measures:** no lobbying or ballot-measure activity of any kind without written founder approval *and* fiscal-sponsor approval *and* legal review. Under a US fiscal sponsor, lobbying limits are governed by the sponsor's 501(c)(3) status; assume the answer is "no" until the sponsor says otherwise in writing. Where a cost driver is policy, Uncost's role is source-led education, open data, and nonpartisan public-interest analysis — never partisan lobbying or campaign work (Plan language, verbatim intent).
4. **Personal capacity:** covered people are free political actors personally, but must not imply Uncost endorsement, use Uncost resources or data, or blend Uncost branding with personal political activity. Sector and region leads must take particular care given their visible roles.
5. **Founder and spokesperson neutrality:** the founder, spokespeople, coordinators, agents, and official channels must not imply Uncost endorsement while acting in an Uncost role. Personal political activity must be clearly separate.
6. **Election-season review:** in the 90 days before a major election in any launch region, all public content referencing legislation, government programs, or officeholders gets a second human review against this policy before publication.
7. **Agents and creators:** automated agents and paid/creator partners are bound by rules 1–2 in all Uncost-branded output; creator agreements must incorporate this policy by reference.

## Escalation
Suspected violations go to the founder (or sponsor compliance contact) immediately; content is unpublished first and adjudicated second.

## Public disclaimer (publish with the policy)
"Uncost is nonpartisan. We critique systems, incentives, and policies with evidence; we do not endorse or oppose candidates or parties."

## Adoption checklist
Legal review of rules 2–3 against the chosen sponsor's rules; add the disclaimer to the website footer and press page; incorporate into creator and volunteer agreements.
